Taking notes helps you not to forget things, teaches you to express yourself, brainstorms your thoughts, research a topic, and so many more things. I used to take notes all my life. Maybe itвҖҷs because IвҖҷm Swiss, they say we are well organised.


I used to write in [OneNote](https://www.ssp.sh/blog/tools-i-use-onenote-part-ii/) for 10+ years. I have notebooks for my bachelor studies and every workplace I worked. I curated a huge notebook with all my business knowledge, and I have a personal one for journaling and organising my personal life.


For a while, I had the desire to switch. There are many reasons. A major one is OneNote isnвҖҷt good anymore with bugs when you search macOS, and the look & feel isnвҖҷt modern anymore. But what is a modern note-taking app in 2021?


## Alternatives or TodayвҖҷs Choice?


A quick [Google Search](https://www.google.com/search?q=note+taking+app&oq=note+taking+app&aqs=chrome..69i57j69i60l2.292j0j9&sourceid=chrome&ie=UTF-8) shows that there are plenty of apps, each promising the world. I tested quite a few of them, and below, you see why I didnвҖҷt go with any of them.

- [Notion.io](https://www.notion.so/) - Write, plan, & get organized in one place

ГўВһВ– No offline mode, slowish feeling and proprietary format
- [Roam](https://roamresearch.com/) - A note-taking tool for networked thought

ГўВһВ– Super expensive, proprietary format
- [Joplin](https://joplinapp.org/) - An open-source note-taking app

ГўВһВ– Perfect, except local file names are using a random hash and sub-folders
- Plain Markdown

ГўВһВ– No support for images, no preview and no notebook container for all your notes


## My Requirements


These are my requirements and might differ from yours. My goal was to own my notes as itвҖҷs my data and my knowledge. Plus, I didnвҖҷt want to migrate from one proprietary format into the next one to lock me in yet another time. Therefore, I wanted to write plain text with the [Markdown Format](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown) and sync them offline to my hard drive.


These requirements put off already a lot of apps. However, I gave none plain-text tools like Notion a chance in case these gave me plenty of joy and multiple excellent features.


A requirement that might be a killer for many of you, which I do not have, is collaborating with others. Sure, I like to share some of my best notes, specific recipes or similar, but I do not need to work together on my journals, know-how-box and other notes. If I want to share a single page, I can export Markdown, generate an HTML/PDF. In case I want an interactive [Coffee List](https://bit.ly/sspaeti_coffee), I use Notion or any [other tools](https://www.ssp.sh/blog/email-and-the-way-we-should-communicate-at-work/#what-are-the-tools-to-do-that) suited.


## The Ultimate Note-Taking App


So what tool did I end using then? What is the ultimate note-taking app in 2021? ItвҖҷs called [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/), and itвҖҷs been a joy ever since. IвҖҷm the complete owner of my notes, and itвҖҷs gorgeously designed.


Now I even have tons of features more, e.g. the [Graph View](https://help.obsidian.md/Plugins/Graph+view) that I didnвҖҷt even know I lacked. But most of all, Obsidian is just a wrapper around your folders and Markdown files, including one single folder called `.obsidian` in your root. ThatвҖҷs it! In case you like to use another editor such as [vim](https://www.vim.org/), [VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/) or just any other [Text Editor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_text_editors) you might have, open all your files individually. Or, in case you want to open all notes in one go, use open Obsidian.

## Price and Features


LetвҖҷs get this out of the way, Obsidian is entirely free! You can add two [paid features](https://obsidian.md/pricing), 1. if you want to use their integrated sync tool or 2. if you want to share your notes, including the fancy graph and outline view.


But yeah, as it is plain text stored on your hard drive, you can use Dropbox, Google Drive or any other sync tool! As IвҖҷm using [Sync.com](https://www.sync.com/) with end-to-end encryption for all my files, it does not work nicely on my mobile. ThatвҖҷs why I added Obsidian Sync. I could have added it to Dropbox, but I didnвҖҷt want to give them my most private notes.


If you want to check out all the other features, see the complete list at [Obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md/) or find more customisation with [25 core](https://help.obsidian.md/Plugins/List+of+plugins) and [329 community plugins](https://obsidian.md/plugins), 60+ themes, plus custom styling, you can tweak Obsidian to work and look exactly how you want it.


Some community plugins do a single thing extremely well, like [Calendar](https://github.com/liamcain/obsidian-calendar-plugin) and [Kanban](https://github.com/mgmeyers/obsidian-kanban). Others unlock endless possibilities: [Dataview](https://github.com/blacksmithgu/obsidian-dataview) and [Templater](https://github.com/SilentVoid13/Templater) are great examples.

### How did I export my 10+ of OneNote to Markdown


In case you are stuck with OneNote as well, I hope these scrips will help you to export them as HTML first and convert them to Markdown so that youвҖҷre the owner of your notes again.


I started with the [Python Script](https://github.com/Danmou/onenote_export) from [Daniel Mouritzen](https://github.com/Danmou) that helped me get started with configuring the Azure API to pull data from it.


I added some additional features that were needed for my kind of OneNote structure. For example, the section-groups were not correctly handled, and I also fixed some errors I got along the wayГўВҖВ”the finalised [onenote_export.py](https://gist.github.com/sspaeti/8daab59a80adc664fa8cbcba707ea21d) you can find on GitHub. The top comments of the script contain a more step-by-step explanation of how to do everything in more detail.


If you run that script until you export all your notebooks, you end up with lots of folders and an HTML file with your notes. HTML is excellent as it keeps the structure of your notes, e.g. if you added two columns or three, the HTML could rebuild that. But on the other hand, they are read-only, which is quite bad for notes as you might want to change, search, combine them later on.


Therefore I created another straightforward Python script [html2markdown.py](https://gist.github.com/sspaeti/73a0fd505f8d30dc09198b148255b4c6) to loop through the folder recursively and convert the HTML to a Markdown file with [markdownify](https://github.com/matthewwithanm/python-markdownify). If youвҖҷre happy with it, you can delete all HTMLвҖҷs, and youвҖҷre done.


Open these folders with Obsidian, and you successfully migrated over to a modern note-taking app in 2021 with hosting the source locally wherever you want.
