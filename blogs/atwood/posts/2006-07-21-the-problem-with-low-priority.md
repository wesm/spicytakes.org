I’ve always thought it was ironic that **low priority emails are the ones I see first in my inbox**.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-466.png)


Marking something with a low priority makes it stand out from all the others. Doesn’t that make it implicitly *high* priority?


[One man’s urgent](http://odetocode.com/Blogs/scott/archive/2006/06/27/4690.aspx) is another man’s low priority, and vice-versa. I think it’s better to leave [the metadata to the machines](https://blog.codinghorror.com/whatever-happened-to-the-meta-tag/) and avoid marking emails with a priority of any kind, high or low.
