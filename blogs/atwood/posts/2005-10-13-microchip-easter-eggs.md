Software engineers aren’t the only ones [planting secret Easter eggs](https://web.archive.org/web/20051026085247/http://www.eeggs.com/tree/153.html) in their products.


Here’s a fun article on the [hidden art etched into microchips](https://web.archive.org/web/20051018025020/http://news.com.com/What+art+is+hiding+on+your+microchip/2100-1006_3-5893374.html). There are a handful of images in the article, but many more at the Silicon Zoo website. Here’s one I found of Mr. T on, appropriately enough, a **T1 transceiver integrated circuit**:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/03/image-309.png)


Notice the medallion says “T1.” Who said hardware engineers didn’t have a sense of humor?


If you want to see more of these hardware in-jokes, visit the [Molecular Expressions: Silicon Zoo page](http://micro.magnet.fsu.edu/creatures/index.html) and scroll down past the interactive Java tutorial image in the middle of the page.
