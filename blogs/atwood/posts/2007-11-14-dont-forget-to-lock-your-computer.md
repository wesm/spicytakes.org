I encourage my coworkers to **lock their computers**. Security, after all, is everyone’s business. But often gentle encouragement is not enough. Sometimes, more... *persuasive* methods are necessary.


I first learned about the noble art of goating from [from Omar Shahine](https://web.archive.org/web/20080916123654/http://www.shahine.com/omar/USBPCLock.aspx):


> We have this problem in Hotmail. If you walk away from your desk, even for a brief moment, and your PC is left unlocked, someone will walk in, and send mail to a broad distribution list with something silly. Like “I like oranges,” or worse things, some downright embarrassing. **For some reason this is called “Goating.”** I find it incredibly annoying. My office has a lock on the door, so I am in the habit of keeping my door locked when I walk away.


Goating techniques vary from insidious and subtle to invasive, borderline vandalism. I prefer the milder forms:

- Installing [the bluescreen screensaver](https://blog.codinghorror.com/the-many-faces-of-windows-death/).
- Replacing the desktop with a screenshot of the desktop, and hiding all the visible items on it.
- Switching the mouse from right to left handed.
- Using the video driver settings to rotate the display left, right, or upside down.
- Switching the keyboard layout from QWERTY to Dvorak (or vice-versa).


Goating can be quite literal. I once walked back to my computer to find this:


![I've been goated!](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/11/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877702086970c-pi.jpg)


It’s disturbingly common here, which is why I’ve learned to reflexively [press Windows+L](https://blog.codinghorror.com/a-celebration-of-the-windows-key/) when I get up from my desk.


One of my all-time favorite goating techniques, however, is to install [the Clippy parody applet](http://www.rjlsoftware.com/software/entertainment/clippy/default.shtml) on a victi... er, coworker’s machine. Who doesn’t love [our old pal Clippy!](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clippy)


![Clippy: I thought you should know today is Wednesday, November 14, 2007.](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/11/6a0120a85dcdae970b01287770208f970c-pi.png)


![Clippy: Your monitor is operational.](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/11/6a0120a85dcdae970b01287770209d970c-pi.png)

After one particularly inspired installation of Clippy, an email titled “What The Heck” went out to all employees:


> Is this another prank or something? What the heck is this? It’s rude.
> Look at the right hand corner of this image.


![Clippy in action!](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/11/6a0120a85dcdae970b0128777020bd970c-pi.jpg)

> So far this stupid thing has told me:
> My typing speed is slow.

Much hilarity ensued, and more importantly, **crucial lessons were learned about computer security by all**.


It’s up to each of us to go forth and spread the good word! If just *one* person learns how important computer security is, your work here is done. Many additional goating techniques can be found in [these](http://ask.metafilter.com/46747/Tell-me-a-good-computer-prank) [two](http://ask.metafilter.com/45744/Boo-I-just-MacPwned-you) metafilter threads; [Office Poltergeist](https://web.archive.org/web/20071113232323/http://officepoltergeist.com/) looks quite promising, as does [ErrMess](https://web.archive.org/web/20061113013311/http://www.errmess.com/products.php). And you really can’t go wrong with Clippy.


But don’t forget to lock *your* computer while you’re out there spreading the word.
