Here’s a data point supporting my hypothesis that users will [jump through any hoops](https://blog.codinghorror.com/full-trust-cant-be-trusted/) to get things for [free as in beer](http://c2.com/cgi/wiki?FreeAsInBeer=). It’s the [SourceForge](http://sourceforge.net/index.php) top downloads list:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/06/image-9.png)


Seven of the ten top downloads are pure p2p file sharing clients. The other three directly relate to media ripping. Somehow I doubt [freedom of speech](http://c2.com/cgi/wiki?FreeAsInSpeech=) is high on most users’ priority lists.


Even running as a non-Administrator account is unlikely to protect users from the fifty percent of shared [executables that contain malicious code](https://web.archive.org/web/20041123091442/http://www.wired.com/news/business/0,1367,61852,00.html).
