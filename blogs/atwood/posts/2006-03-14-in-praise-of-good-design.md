Which pill bottle would you rather use?


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-230.png)


The rightmost bottle was designed by Target to address the shortcomings of traditional pill bottles. And you probably decided which pill bottle you liked best within [a twentieth of a second](http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/technology/4616700.stm).


When I suggested [redesigning address input](https://blog.codinghorror.com/doing-it-like-everyone-else-does/) in web forms, there was some resistance. Current web forms are *good enough*. Yeah, they have some shortcomings. But why change what works? Why change what people are used to?


**Why did we have to wait 50 years for a better pill bottle?** Because the shortcomings of pill bottles weren’t *important* enough to justify a new design? I think that’s a crock. We should constantly be striving to overcome existing shortcomings with better designs.


Donald Norman has a page with examples of [well-designed products](https://web.archive.org/web/20060404042845/http://www.jnd.org/GoodDesign.html) that he calls **In Praise of Good Design**. Did the kitchen whisk *need* to be redesigned? No. Is it better *because* it was redesigned? Absolutely.
