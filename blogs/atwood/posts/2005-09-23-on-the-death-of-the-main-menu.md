One of the biggest highlights of PDC 2005 was the first day keynote, when the Office 12 UI was unveiled. I don’t know if people realized the significance of what we saw at the time – but **we had just witnessed the death of the main menu**.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/03/image-282.png)


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/03/image-281.png)


There’s no “dropping down” in Office 12;* it’s a context-sensitive tabbed palette interface.


**There is no main menu.**


That’s huge. The main menu has been a cornerstone of the [WIMP interface](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WIMP_%28computing%29) since way back in the days of the [Apple Lisa](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Lisa). Julie Larson-Green, the PM of the office UI team, [explains](https://web.archive.org/web/20051001014119/http://www.microsoft.com/presspass/features/2005/sep05/09-13OfficeUI.mspx):


> *The main part of the user experience is code-named the “ribbon.” It’s the one place you go to find the commands that are all about authoring – creating the document, the presentation or the spreadsheet you’re working on. There’s no longer a stack of task panes and menus and toolbars to look through. There’s just one place to look for commands.*


UI innovations in Office tend to be rapidly adopted by Microsoft across their entire product line. Not only there, but in third party applications and even other operating systems. **Remember the toolbar?** That was unknown until it debuted in Word in the early 90s.** Now it’s ubiquitous. The “ribbon” is a similar paradigm shift. Eventually we’ll all be using these tabbed palettes with nary a drop-down menu in sight. I expect the traditional WIMP main menu to go the way of the dodo soon after the release of Vista and Office 12 in 2006.


*With the apparent exception of the File drop-down, but even that doesn’t look like a menu in the traditional sense when you click on it.


**The [MS presspass article](https://web.archive.org/web/20051001014119/http://www.microsoft.com/presspass/features/2005/sep05/09-13OfficeUI.mspx) states that this is “the most dramatic change to the way Office apps work since the introduction of the toolbar in 1997.” **I think the toolbar goes back much further than that!** I just installed a copy of Microsoft Word 1.1a in a fresh Windows 3.1 VM image. Guess what? [It has a toolbar](https://web.archive.org/web/20061130100247/http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/images/microsoft_word_v1.png). So does Word 2.0... and [Excel 4.0](https://web.archive.org/web/20061130162535/http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/images/microsoft%20excel_v4.png). I don’t know what kind of crack those MS presspass guys are smoking...
