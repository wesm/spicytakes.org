An eagle-eyed CodeProject reader posted a comment to my [ASP.NET CAPTCHA Server Control](https://blog.codinghorror.com/an-aspnet-captcha-server-control/) article pointing out one French developer’s very thorough attempts to [defeat common CAPTCHA techniques](http://sam.zoy.org/projects/pwntcha/). He compares lots of visual CAPTCHAs side by side and comments on their strengths and weaknesses. Some of the “best” CAPTCHAs have this comment attached to them: *not always human-solvable.* Heh.


There’s also a [W3C presentation from Matt May](http://www.w3.org/2004/Talks/0319-csun-m3m/slide1-0.html) which attempts to discredit CAPTCHAs entirely, though I think **their increasing prevalence is ample proof that they actually work.** If nothing else, they raise the bar much, much higher for spambots. His main point seems to be that CAPTCHAs hurt accessibility, and that’s totally valid.


What the heezy?!
