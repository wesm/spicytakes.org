A recent post by Steve Makofsky reminded me that the excellent UI Patterns and Techniques site is now a book from O'Reilly – [Designing Interfaces](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0596008031).


There’s technically no reason to buy a book on visual design patterns when you can [find the same information online](https://blog.codinghorror.com/is-ui-still-in-the-stone-age/)...

- [GUI design patterns](https://web.archive.org/web/20060206134340/http://www.welie.com/)
- [UI Patterns and Techniques](https://web.archive.org/web/20060125012722/http://time-tripper.com/uipatterns/)
- [The GUI Gallery](http://toastytech.com/guis/)
- [The GUIdebook](http://www.guidebookgallery.org/)


... but what a glorious, infinitely browsable **full-color **book this is. It’s highly visual and truly does justice to the concept: a [Design Patterns](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0201633612) for the eye instead of the mind. Sometimes atoms *are *better than bytes.


Unlike any of the Design Patterns in that famous book, these patterns are [visible to your users](https://blog.codinghorror.com/gui-patterns/). Plan accordingly.
