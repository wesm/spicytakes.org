I accepted a position at [Vertigo Software](https://web.archive.org/web/20050522081417/http://www.vertigosoftware.com/) today.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-83.png)


You may know Vertigo from one or more of the following:

- [Quake II .NET](https://web.archive.org/web/20050603080424/http://www.vertigosoftware.com/Quake2.htm) (Managed C++)
- IBuySpy (ASP.NET)
- .NET Pet Shop (J2EE performance comparison)
- [Fitch & Mather](https://web.archive.org/web/20050831171604/http://www.fmstocks.com/default.asp) (Windows DNA)
- [FotoVision](https://web.archive.org/web/20050530234743/http://msdn.microsoft.com/smartclient/codesamples/FotoVision/default.aspx), [IssueVision](https://web.archive.org/web/20050712083817/http://www.windowsforms.net/Applications/application.aspx?PageID=40&tabindex=9) (.NET Smart Client)


I’m tremendously excited to join such a high-profile group of software developers, and I can’t wait to start. I don’t actually arrive at work until Thursday, May 26, because it takes time to deal with the move from NC to CA:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-84.png)


I’ve mentioned Vertigo in a few of my blog posts before, [Managed Code Performance](https://blog.codinghorror.com/on-managed-code-performance/) and [Weeding out the Weak Developers with J2EE](https://blog.codinghorror.com/weeding-out-the-weak-developers-with-j2ee/). In the latter study, the three developers on the .NET team were all from Vertigo.
