Although [I prefer IE7’s native](https://blog.codinghorror.com/non-native-ui-sucks/) speed and feel for day-to-day browsing chores, there’s no doubt that **Firefox is my primary web development IDE**.


Whenever I need to troubleshoot HTML, CSS, or JavaScript, I immediately reach for Firefox. That’s primarily because of two incredibly powerful developer extensions for Firefox:


**1. **[**Web Developer Extension**](http://chrispederick.com/work/webdeveloper/)


![Firefox Web Developer Extension screenshot](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/04/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877700667970c-pi.png)


WDE has lots of useful features, but the most wonderful thing about WDE is that **everything is editable in real-time**. Edit the CSS, edit the HTML, and watch your changes take effect as you type. It’s like magic.


**2. **[**Firebug**](http://www.getfirebug.com/)


![Firefox Firebug screenshot](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/04/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877700673970c-pi.png)


Firebug is a richer tool than WDE; it goes far beyond real-time editing of HTML and CSS, and delves deeply into profiling and debugging JavaScript. It also offers metrics on download time and download order over the wire. I highly recommend checking out the Joe Hewitt [Firebug power user demo movie](https://web.archive.org/web/20071011055434/http://ajaxian.com/archives/joe-hewitt-firebug-power-user-demo) to get a sense of its full feature set.


I can’t imagine debugging a web app without these two essential tools, which **transform Firefox into the Visual Studio of browsers**. It’d be akin to [writing a .NET application](https://blog.codinghorror.com/its-the-ide-dummy/) in Notepad. In fact, I feel so strongly about the utility of these extensions that I’m stopping to donate $10 to each project, right now. This is part of my ongoing monthly [contribution to small software vendors](https://blog.codinghorror.com/today-is-support-your-favorite-small-software-vendor-day/).


If you haven’t used these tools yet, I envy the experience you’re about to have. Download them both and start experimenting. If you have used Firebug and WDE, I encourage you to share your favorite tips in the comments.
