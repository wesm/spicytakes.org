I was struck, the other day, by how much [I had to *think*](https://blog.codinghorror.com/dont-make-me-think-second-edition/) when attempting to heat up my sandwich in the microwave. There are so many controls: a clock, a set of food-specific buttons, defrost and timer controls, and of course a full numeric keypad. Quick! What do you press?


![Controls on a newer microwave](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/10/6a0120a85dcdae970b0120a86dacdf970b-pi.jpg)


![Controls on an older microwave](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/10/6a0120a85dcdae970b0120a86dacf6970b-pi.jpg)


I wonder if older microwaves weren’t [a better, simpler design](https://blog.codinghorror.com/simplicity-as-a-force/), with their single giant analog knob. I noticed that every microwave for sale at Target, even the most inexpensive ones, now use a complete set of digital controls.


This is progress?


Maybe something to think about, the next time you’re about to add [just one more field](https://blog.codinghorror.com/but-its-just-one-more/), [button](https://blog.codinghorror.com/googles-number-one-ui-mistake/), or [link](https://blog.codinghorror.com/dont-click-here-the-art-of-hyperlinking/) to that form.
