A reader recently pointed out that the second edition of [Don’t Make Me Think](https://bookshop.org/p/books/designing-map-interfaces-patterns-for-building-effective-map-apps-michael-gaigg/af89ed645548004e?ean=9780321965516&next=t) is about to be released. I know I’ve pimped this book ad nauseam, but I can’t help myself – it’s just that good. **If you only read one book on usability and UI, it should be Krug’s Don’t Make Me Think.** I even gave it to my wife to read, and she enjoyed it too. Is there any higher praise?


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-132.png)


Unfortunately, Steve Krug’s website doesn’t have any information on [what’s new in this version](https://web.archive.org/web/20051208030918/http://www.sensible.com:80/secondedition/index.html). The yellow sidebar indicates “3 new chapters,” but beyond that, who knows.


(I've updated this post to link to the third and final version, [Don't Make Me Think, Revisited](https://bookshop.org/p/books/designing-map-interfaces-patterns-for-building-effective-map-apps-michael-gaigg/af89ed645548004e?ean=9780321965516&next=t))


I’ll be getting a copy of the new version for sure. I can keep the old one at home and the new one at work, or I can surprise an unsuspecting manager or fellow developer with the first edition. Now if only I could get them to *read* it...
