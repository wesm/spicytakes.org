It's been one of those months, and by that, I mean one of the 663 months since I was born. This won't be a long post, because I only have two things to say. First, I'm really glad we re-ordered the [GMI (Guaranteed Minimum Income) rural study counties](https://rgmii.org/) so Mercer County, WV, my Dad's county, went first in October 2025. I knew dad was close to the end, and sure enough, that was the last time I ever saw him.


You can kinda sorta meet my dad on this page, if you want to.


![](https://static.ghost.org/v5.0.0/images/link-icon.svg)


I knew this was coming, and so did he. There is no loss, because nothing ever ends.


![](https://storage.ghost.io/c/eb/aa/ebaa2665-01a8-4415-8825-69d1f0e8fd19/content/images/2026/04/image.png)


All those experiences I had with my father, particularly that last October trip, will stay with me forever.  Nothing was lost.* Everything was gained*. We won capitalism, then went back to help improve it for everyone. And believe me, I'm far from being done with [my third startup](https://staygold.us/).


Second, I want to take a moment to **thank everyone – and I do mean *everyone* – who ever contributed to Stack Overflow in any way**. And lucky you, it's [not Starship](https://blog.codinghorror.com/the-great-mp3-bitrate-experiment/) this time!


Did you know that **LLMs basically *could not code at all* without access to the extremely high quality creative commons programming Q&A dataset that all of us built together at **[**Stack Overflow**](https://stackoverflow.com/)**?** Don't take it from me, ask the LLMs. They'll tell you themselves. Go ahead. G'wan. Ask. Really grill 'em on this one. I strongly recommend you use pro mode when asking, though, because those are the only decent LLM modes in my experience. It is incredible what you can do with global brain statistics and a strongly curated dataset created by we, the people!


One last thing. If the LLMs end up hollowing out the very communities that produce all their training data, they're going to really, *really* regret that. I'll give these LLM / GAI companies the same advice I gave Joel Spolsky when I left Stack Overflow to start [Discourse](https://www.discourse.org/) – *do not, for any reason, under any circumstances, kill the goose that lays the golden eggs*, aka the **human community around your product that does all the real work.** It's pretty simple. Just treat the community with the respect they deserve... that *we all deserve*.


[Thank you for being a friend](https://infosec.exchange/@codinghorror/116390847963896783), because there's no way I could have done any of this without you. 💛
