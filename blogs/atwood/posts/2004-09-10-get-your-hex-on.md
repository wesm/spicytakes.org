It’s kind of a specialized tool, but when you need it,* a **hex editor** is indispensable. I’ve used [Hex Workshop](http://www.hexworkshop.com/screen_shots.html) since, geez, 1997! I recently purchased an upgrade to the latest version and I was pleased to see it’s still under very active development, with lots of great new features:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/06/image-38.png)


One of the more interesting new features is the **structure viewer**. I have a zip file open in the above screenshot; notice the zip file structure in the bottom left, with the filename highlighted. You can actually do in-place editing on the structures, and of course, define your own. There’s also a fairly sophisticated resynchronizing **binary file comparison** mode, too.


*I’m currently revisiting some MP3 ID3 tagging code; I need to look closely at the structure of the tags in the binary fine. Loverboy? Hey, I never said I had good taste in music.
