Via [Ole Eichhorn](http://w-uh.com/), the UK Guardian’s [Survival of the Unfittest](http://technology.guardian.co.uk/weekly/story/0,,1705106,00.html):


> *Lotus Notes is used by millions of people, but almost all of them seem to hate it. How can a program be so bad, yet thrive?*


We’ve all had bad software experiences. However, at one of my jobs, our corporate email client of choice was Lotus Notes. And **until you’ve used Lotus Notes, you haven’t truly experienced bad software.** It is death by a thousand tiny annoyances – the digital equivalent of being kicked in the groin upon arrival at work every day.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-198.png)


I won’t say that Lotus Notes was the reason I quit that job, but it was definitely a factor in my decision. [The UI is the application.](https://blog.codinghorror.com/the-user-interface-is-the-application/)


Lotus Notes is a train wreck of epic (enterprise?) proportions, but it might be worth studying to **learn what not to do when developing software**.
