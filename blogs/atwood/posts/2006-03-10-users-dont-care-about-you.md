Seth Godin showed this slide during a recent presentation at Google:


> Users don’t care about YOU.


What’s the biggest [web design mistake of 2004?](http://www.webpagesthatsuck.com/biggest-web-design-mistakes-in-2004.html)


> 1. Believing people care about you and your web site.


Why isn’t anyone reading our blog?


> 1. You’re not a good-looking female who likes [posting naked pictures of herself](http://www.samugliestdog.com/pastwinners.html).
> 2. There’s nothing in it for them.


This is not to say that [users don’t care](http://headrush.typepad.com/creating_passionate_users/2006/02/rethinking_test.html):


> The main point: **users don’t care about you as much as they care about themselves.**


Users just [aren’t that into you](http://headrush.typepad.com/creating_passionate_users/2005/01/users_shouldnt_.html). It’s not personal. It’s business. Unless what you’re writing…

- solves their problem
- provides useful information
- entertains them
- makes them feel like [they rule](http://headrush.typepad.com/creating_passionate_users/2005/10/making_happy_us.html)


… it’s irrational to expect users to care about it.


Every time you create something – whether it’s a website, a client app, a blog post, a PowerPoint presentation, or an email – ask yourself, “so what?” If you can’t answer that question convincingly, reformulate and try again.
