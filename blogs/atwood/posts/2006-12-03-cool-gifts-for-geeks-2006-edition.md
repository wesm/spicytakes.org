This year, I preemptively treated myself to a the new [4 gigabyte iRiver Clix](https://web.archive.org/web/20061205221837/http://www.iriveramerica.com/prod/ultra/clix/clix-4GB.aspx) ($199), and after living with this stellar little flash music player for a week, I can heartily recommend it to anyone.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/06/image-130.png)


It does everything [the iPod refuses to do](https://blog.codinghorror.com/ipod-alternatives/): subscription services, wma/ogg support, FM Radio (w/recording!), Flash Lite games, video playback, and voice recording. It has a speedy, intuitive UI that can be switched between landscape or portrait mode at will. And best of all, no software installs are required:* just plug it in, drag and drop your music on it, then play. But don’t take my word for it: read the glowing critical acclaim at [PC Magazine](https://web.archive.org/web/20070502043646/http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,1895,1961584,00.asp), [ABiP](https://web.archive.org/web/20070106133225/http://www.anythingbutipod.com/archives/2006/05/iriver-clix-review.php), and [Cnet](https://web.archive.org/web/20070225050432/http://reviews.cnet.com/iRiver_Clix_2GB/4505-6490_7-31861628-2.html).


I’ve provided one specific gift recommendation, and my favorite geek gift suggestion lists so far. But I’m sure I’ve missed plenty. **Are there any cool geek gift lists I’ve missed?**


*Caveat: assuming you have an OS that supports [MTP](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_Transfer_Protocol).
