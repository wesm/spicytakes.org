Maybe it’s just my [OCD kicking in again](http://www.ocfoundation.org/), but it’s incredibly annoying how these phantom, zero-byte IO.SYS and MSDOS.SYS files keep showing up in the root of my c: drive on every computer I own.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-104.png)


It’s a gentle reminder of the Bad Old Days. The last time I checked, I wasn’t running DOS 6.22, Win95, Win98SE, or even WinME. **No matter how many times I delete these spurious DOS-related files, the next time I look – they’re back**. What’s next? [AUTOEXEC.BAT and CONFIG.SYS](http://www.computerhope.com/ac.htm)? HIMEM.SYS and optimizing my high memory area? Argh.
