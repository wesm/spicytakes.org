God bless whoever at Microsoft decided to build [Calculator Plus](http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/details.aspx?familyid=32b0d059-b53a-4dc9-8265-da47f157c091&displaylang=en&ref=blog.codinghorror.com), an unsupported free upgrade for calc.exe. On the other hand... who decided it was a good idea to skin the UI by default?


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/06/image-40.png)


[**My eyes! The goggles, they do nothing!**](https://web.archive.org/web/20041009231011/http://www.snpp.com/episodes/2F17.html) Now compare that “upgraded” UI to the windows default, which is thankfully still selectable via the View menu:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/06/image-41.png)


Perhaps boring, but far more usable. I know skinning is a matter of opinion to some degree, but – come on. At least have a professional graphic designer put together a skin if you’re going to traumatize the user with a non-standard UI. And even then, I doubt you should be doing it. You better have a damn good reason to deviate from the standard OS look and feel that users expect. For comparison, Windows Media Center does have a good reason to implement a radically different UI – it’s designed to be used from the couch with a remote.
