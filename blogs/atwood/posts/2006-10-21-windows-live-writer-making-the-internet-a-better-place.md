Does this look familiar?


> Temporary Post Used For Style Detection (14debf21-5e75-4077-9bf0-88d425739dc7)
> This is a temporary post that was not deleted. Please delete this manually. (f19173c9-9b1f-4430-8823-bae7c95236a0)


**Seriously. Enough with this already.** I’m gonna hurt somebody.


If you’re not in on the joke, it’s an artifact of Microsoft’s [Windows Live Writer](https://web.archive.org/web/20061018065215/http://windowslivewriter.spaces.live.com/blog/cns!D85741BB5E0BE8AA!174.entry) blogging tool. It generates “hidden” skeleton blog posts to detect CSS styles in its editor, but these posts somehow keep showing up in my aggregator. And *that’s annoying*.


There are also 19,500 google results for the phrase “[temporary post used for style detection](http://www.google.com/search?q=%22temporary+post+used+for+style+detection%22).” Gee, thanks, Windows Live Writer. That’s exactly 19,499 too many. [This post is actually useful](http://labnol.blogspot.com/2006/08/microsoft-wysiwyg-blog-editor-how-do-i.html), since it describes the problem in more detail.


On the plus side, I did get the chance to bring two new GUIDs into the world. And you know how [I love GUIDs](https://blog.codinghorror.com/mastering-guids-with-occams-razor/).
