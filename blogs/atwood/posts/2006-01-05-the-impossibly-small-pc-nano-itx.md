VIA’s Nano-ITX fits an entire PC motherboard into an **impossibly small 12 by 12 centimeter format**:


![nano_itx-256.png](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2006/01/6a0120a85dcdae970b0128776fd505970c-pi.png)


This board has been gestating for a while at VIA, and according to [the nanoitx blog](http://nanoitx.blogspot.com/), it’s evidently because of interference and heat problems presented by the extremely small form factor. But a retail board was [sighted in Japan’s Akiba district](http://www.mini-itx.com/news/10246161/) in late November, so it can’t be too far off now.

If you’ve ever purchased a motherboard, chances are it was standard ATX size. The ITX specifications are essentially a VIA creation, but detailed information on the other form factors is available at [formfactors.org](https://web.archive.org/web/20060110160450/http://www.formfactors.org/formfactor.asp)


The Nano-ITX will be available in two versions: fanless 800 mhz for ~$300 and a 1 ghz version that requires a small fan for ~$350. You’d need a few more pieces to make a complete mini-PC, though:

- 40gb 2.5″ IDE drive, $65
- 512mb SODIMM, $45
- Slim DVD drive, $45
- Nano-ITX case and power supply, ~$100 (?)


**It’d be easy to build a PC smaller than a Mac Mini using a Nano-ITX board**. Just don’t expect a lot of power; the VIA CPUs aren’t exactly barn-burners. The VIA boards are fine for basic web browsing and productivity tasks with undemanding users, but if you need more than that, you’ll want a [Pentium M Mini-ITX motherboard](https://web.archive.org/web/20061118065656/http://www.epiacenter.com/modules.php?name=News&file=article&sid=374).
