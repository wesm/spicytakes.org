I arrived at work today to find this package.


![package mailer](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877700d2e970c-pi.jpg)


It’s from one “C. Petzold,” [whoever the heck that is](https://blog.codinghorror.com/the-cognitive-style-of-visual-studio/).


Inside was a copy of the book [3D Programming for Windows](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0735623945): Three-Dimensional Graphics Programming for the Windows Presentation Foundation.


![3D Programming for Windows: Three-Dimensional Graphics Programming for the Windows Presentation Foundation](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877700d36970c-pi.jpg)


It’s even inscribed:


![book inscription: Jeff, Hope you enjoy this one! (And I hope I didn't make too many coding or literary horrors.)](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b012877700d41970c-pi.jpg)


This is, of course, a reference to my post [How Not To Write a Technical Book](https://blog.codinghorror.com/how-not-to-write-a-technical-book/), in which I rather harshly called Petzold’s [previous book](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0735619573) “a greyscale sea of endless text and interminable code,” and compared it very unfavorably with [Adam Nathan’s book](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0672328917) on the same topic.


I can’t speak for the quality of the book, as I haven’t had time to read it yet. But if nothing else, it tells us that Charles Petzold has a good sense of humor. (And, indirectly, it demonstrates the value of an [“about me” page](https://blog.codinghorror.com/thirteen-blog-cliches/) on your blog with your contact information.)


Thanks, Mr. Petzold.
