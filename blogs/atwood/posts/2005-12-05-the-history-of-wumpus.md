It was one of my formative computing experiences on the first “real” computer I owned. No, [my Coleco ADAM doesn’t count](https://blog.codinghorror.com/the-cult-of-coleco-adam/).


The images here refer to the The [TI 99/4a cartridge version of Hunt The Wumpus](http://web.archive.org/web/20100226221837/http://www.videogamehouse.net/huntwumpus.html) from about 1985.


However, Hunt The Wumpus [dates back much further](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunt_the_Wumpus). Here’s the [original 1975 BASIC version of Hunt the Wumpus](https://web.archive.org/web/20100226221837/http://www.atariarchives.org/bcc1/showpage.php?page=247), by Gregory Yob.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-543.png)


There’s even a multiplayer version online.


A colleague was kind enough to port the original 1975 basic code to C#. You can [download the .NET version of Hunt The Wumpus ](https://github.com/coding-horror/wumpus-net). This version has a few modern amenities, such as representing the cave maps in XML files instead of DATA statements, but it preserves the UI as-is. Here’s a screenshot of the magical gameplay experience that awaits you:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-544.png)


If you use your wits and your arrows judiciously, you might f[i](https://www.redbubble.com/shop/ap/168850245)nd that Wumpus one day.
