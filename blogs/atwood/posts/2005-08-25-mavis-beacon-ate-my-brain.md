You may be familiar with the classic [Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing](http://www.google.com/search?q=mavis+beacon+teaches+typing)* series of software from Broderbund.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/08/mavis-beacon-teaches-typing-1987.jpg)


You play it interactively courtesy of the [always amazing Internet Archive](https://blog.codinghorror.com/preserving-the-internet-and-everything-else/).


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/icon/glogo.jpg)


Well, Sega’s sublime [Typing of the Dead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Typing_of_the_Dead) is like Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing*... if Mavis Beacon was a flesh-eating zombie!!*


Any self-respecting software developer should be a [decent typist](https://blog.codinghorror.com/we-are-typists-first-programmers-second/). Now you can prove how much of a [keyboard ninja](https://theonion.com/disgruntled-ninja-silently-kills-12-co-workers-1819565345/) you really are, because here’s the best part: **Typing of The Dead supports 2-player competitive network play**, with comprehensive post-game stat tracking.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/08/typing-of-the-dead-multiplayer-end-stats.jpg)


It’s hard to explain just how wondrously bizarre Typing of the Dead is. It’s a challenging, remarkably well thought out typing tutor, a tongue-in-cheek b-grade zombie movie, and the most hilarious multiplayer game experience you’ll probably ever have – all at the same time.


As a zombie enthusiast, I bought Typing of the Dead immediately after it was released in 2001, but it's probably easier to play the 2013 update, [The Typing of the Dead: Overkill](https://store.steampowered.com/app/246580/The_Typing_of_The_Dead_Overkill/).


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/icon/favicon-12.ico)


Now sit back and watch the hilarity ensue. It’s pure genius.


* Interestingly, [Mavis Beacon isn’t a real person](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mavis_Beacon_(character)). She’s an [imaginary identity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pUrO0blAJOk) created entirely for the purpose of marketing. So I guess that puts her somewhere in between, say, [Peter Norton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Norton) and [Carmen Sandiego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carmen_Sandiego_(character)).
