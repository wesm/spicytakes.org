I recently found a link to a series of [Larry Gonick’s mathematical cartoons](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/) that were originally published in Discover magazine:

- [Beauty and the Beasts](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074402/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/01.htm) (neural nets)
- [Prime Time](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074429/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/03.htm) (cryptography)
- [Proof Positive?](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/05.htm) (probabilistic proofs)
- [Lumps, with Mother Nature](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/07.htm) (chaotic mixing)
- [Speed](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/09.htm) (relativity)
- [The Solution](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/11.htm) (traveling salesman's problem)
- [Filler Up!](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/13.htm) (space-filling solids)
- [The Bowerbird's Dilemma](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/15.htm) (game theory)
- [El Topo](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/17.htm) (DNA topology)
- [Portrait of a Problem](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/19.htm) (protein-folding energy landscapes)
- [It's the Pits](https://web.archive.org/web/20060423074335/http://www.msri.org/ext/larryg/pages/21.htm) (wavelets)


It’s great stuff. Here’s a sample panel from the one on cryptography:

I’ve followed [Larry Gonick’s](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larry_Gonick) work for ages. He’s famous for his easy to grasp, beautifully illustrated treatment of complex topics – as exemplified in the series of books he’s published:

- [Cartoon History of the Universe Vol I](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0385265204) (1990)
- [Cartoon History of the Universe Vol II](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0385420935) (1994)
- [Cartoon History of the Universe Vol III](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0393324036) (2002)
- [Cartoon History of the Modern World Vol I](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0060760044) (ETA late 2006)
- [Cartoon History of the United States](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062730983) (1991)
- [Cartoon Guide to Chemistry](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0060936770) (2005)
- [Cartoon Guide to the Computer](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0064604179) (1983)
- [Cartoon Guide to the Environment](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062732749) (1996)
- [Cartoon Guide to Genetics](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062730991) (1991)
- [Cartoon Guide to (non)Communication](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/006273217X) (1993)
- [Cartoon Guide to Physics](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062731009) (1992)
- [Cartoon Guide to Sex](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062734318) (1999)
- [Cartoon Guide to Statistics](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062731025) (1994)


Although I made fun of the [Learn ASP.NET in 24 Hours](https://blog.codinghorror.com/teach-yourself-programming-in-23-hours/) meme, **I am a big fan of learning subjects in cartoon form**. You learn more when you’re having fun doing it.
