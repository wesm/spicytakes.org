I’ve been traveling a bit more lately, which reminded me to update my list of [recommended laptop travel accessories](https://blog.codinghorror.com/road-warrior-come-out-to-play/).


Although I was happy with the [Microsoft Notebook Optical Mouse](https://web.archive.org/web/20070204033049/http://www.microsoft.com/hardware/mouseandkeyboard/productdetails.aspx?pid=070), the snap-in power off switch never worked very well for me. As a result, it got switched on a few times in my laptop bag, leaving me with a dead mouse when I needed it. So I needed a **new notebook mouse**. I switched to the Logitech V500:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-302.png)


It’s got a neat pop-up panel on the bottom which serves three functions:

1. it’s a power switch
2. it provides a larger, angled profile for comfort
3. it serves as a secure storage compartment for the wireless USB receiver


The solid-state scrolling surface (instead of a wheel) takes a little getting used to, but it works well, and there’s a satisfying audible click that plays as you “scroll.” It takes up less space than any of the Microsoft notebook mice, too.


If you’ve ever been trapped at a crowded airport with all the power outlets occupied by opportunistic travelers, you’ll wish you had brought **a mini power strip** with you. The [Monster Cable Outlets to Go](https://web.archive.org/web/20060614110711/http://www.monstercable.com/power/lineOutlets.asp) mini power strip looks like it will fit the bill nicely:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-303.png)


Too bad it’s not available yet. If you can’t wait, most hardware stores carry the good old three-port power outlet expander:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-304.png)


I also occasionally need to **network multiple PCs on the road**. Given the prices of today’s routers, there’s absolutely no reason to carry something that’s merely a switch or hub. Consider the [Linksys WRT54GC compact wireless router](https://web.archive.org/web/20060612215353/http://www.linksys.com/servlet/Satellite?c=L_Product_C2&childpagename=US%2FLayout&cid=1115416825655&pagename=Linksys%2FCommon%2FVisitorWrapper), which I picked up on eBay for under $40 shipped:


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-305.png)


The newer [Wireless-G Travel Router](https://web.archive.org/web/20060627060109/http://www.linksys.com/servlet/Satellite?c=L_Product_C2&childpagename=US%2FLayout&cid=1122062241008&pagename=Linksys%2FCommon%2FVisitorWrapper) is even smaller, and it plugs directly into the wall, obviating the need for any external power brick.


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-306.png)


However, it only has one client ethernet port, so it’s not suitable as a wired switch. That’s kind of a bummer.


I still recommend (and use) my [external USB wireless antenna](https://web.archive.org/web/20060614072719/http://www.hawkingtech.com/products/productlist.php?CatID=32&FamID=60&ProdID=280), and a retractable cable kit. Oh yeah, and the [portable laptop stand](https://blog.codinghorror.com/road-warrior-beyond-lapdom/) can be a lifesaver, too.
