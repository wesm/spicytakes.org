In the spirit of Jennifer Tidwell’s [excellent Designing Interfaces book](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0596008031), there are a few great catalogs of data visualization emerging online.


Start with the oft-cited [**Periodic Table of Visualization Methods**](http://www.visual-literacy.org/periodic_table/periodic_table.html).


![Periodic Table of Visualization Methods](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b0120a86d8eda970b-pi.png)


There’s another excellent collection at [**Data Visualization: Modern Approaches**](http://www.smashingmagazine.com/2007/08/02/data-visualization-modern-approaches/).


![visualization, html graph](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b0120a86d8efc970b-pi.png)


![visualization, munterbund](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/uploads/2007/08/6a0120a85dcdae970b0120a86d8f21970b-pi.jpg)


If you’re looking for visualization with a less practical, more web-oriented bent, a colleague recently discovered the [FWA: Favourite Web Awards](http://www.thefwa.com/) site. It’s a huge catalog of websites that use interesting, unique designs and visualizations. That’s where we found [the Uniqlock “clock,”](https://web.archive.org/web/20070824091116/http://www.uniqlo.jp/uniqlock/) and the [giant rickshaw pointer](https://web.archive.org/web/20070809105341/http://www.1-click.jp/#0.0306590928107231).


At any rate, if you’re [a student of Tufte](https://blog.codinghorror.com/reading-with-edward-tufte/) like I am, you might find it helpful to review a sample of what visualizations and techniques are possible (or even advisable) before plowing ahead on [your next “Rich Internet Application.”](http://redmonk.com/cote/2007/07/12/update-from-ria-land-air-silverlight-what-is-an-ria-better-guis-the-killer-app/)
