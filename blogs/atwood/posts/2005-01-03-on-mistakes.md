It’s always reassuring to be reminded that people much more talented than myself make mistakes, too. And I especially appreciate it when they share those mistakes in the form of advice:


On avoiding IT mistakes: Rick Cattell’s, [Things I Wish I Learned in Engineering School](http://www.cattell.net/talk/TiwiliesTalk.htm):

kg-card-begin: html

> Good technology is only 10% of “real world” success
> It’s frustrating to spend years of your career building things that aren’t used
> Maybe you can avoid some of my mistakes

kg-card-end: html

On avoiding creative mistakes: Hugh Macleod’s, [How to Be Creative](https://web.archive.org/web/20050106085037/http://www.gapingvoid.com/Moveable_Type/archives/000876.html):

kg-card-begin: html

> *
> Ignore everybody.
> The idea doesn’t have to be big. 
> It just has to change the world.
> Put the hours in.
> If your biz plan depends on you suddenly being “discovered” by some big shot, your plan will probably fail.
> You are responsible for your own experience.
> *

kg-card-end: html
