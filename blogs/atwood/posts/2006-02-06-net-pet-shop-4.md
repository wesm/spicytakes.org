Vertigo Software’s .NET Pet Shop 4.0 article just went live on MSDN.


It’s Pet Shop! You know... our old pal, [Pet Shop](https://web.archive.org/web/20060209030812/http://www.gotdotnet.com/team/compare/petshop.aspx):


![](https://blog.codinghorror.com/content/images/2025/05/image-173.png)


However, unlike previous versions of Pet Shop, **this version is not a benchmark comparison with Java**. It’s purely a showcase for ASP.NET 2.0 features and best practices when porting a site from ASP.NET 1.1.


The lineage of Pet Shop is a little hard to trace, but it goes something like this:

kg-card-begin: html


| 1.x | ASP.NET 1.0 | Direct port of [Java Pet Store](https://web.archive.org/web/20060407030233/http://java.sun.com/developer/releases/petstore/) reference application. |
| 2.x | ASP.NET 1.1 | Minor update for new ASP.NET 1.1 release. |
| 3.x | ASP.NET 1.1 | Incorporates community feedback; improved architecture to better align with 
      [Microsoft Patterns & Practices](https://web.archive.org/web/20060209020342/http://msdn.microsoft.com/practices/) recommendations. |
| 4.x | ASP.NET 2.0 | Complete rewrite; no longer functionally equivalent to Java reference application. |


kg-card-end: html

Although Pet Shop was developed here at Vertigo over the last 8 months, I wasn’t personally involved with Pet Shop in any capacity. We are definitely interested in your feedback, however. Dialog with the community is what drove Pet Shop 3.0 development. [Alan](https://web.archive.org/web/20060224204104/http://blogs.vertigosoftware.com/alanl/default.aspx) created a public blog entry on the [Vertigo Pet Shop blog](https://web.archive.org/web/20060213223153/http://blogs.vertigosoftware.com/petshop/default.aspx) soliciting comments; feel free to drop by and comment any time.
