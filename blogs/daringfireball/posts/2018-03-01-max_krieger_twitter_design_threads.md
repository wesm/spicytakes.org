A few nights ago I stumbled upon an excellent [thread on Twitter](https://twitter.com/MaxKriegerVG/status/968609994764705792) by Max Krieger on the design of Sony’s ambitious but ill-fated Metreon complex in San Francisco. If you’ve ever been to Moscone for a conference, you know the Metreon — it’s the big weird mall across the street from Moscone West.


I found several things interesting about this:

- Twitter threads can be annoying at times, compared to reading a regular old article. But when done well, they’re engaging. Krieger is a natural at the format — breaking all his thoughts into tweet-sized chunks and including plenty of photos illustrating his points. And Twitter clients are good at displaying multiple images in a carousel.
- Krieger used the thread to promote [his Kickstarter campaign for a puzzle game](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1113141213/crossniq) he’s making. [As Ben Thompson noted](https://twitter.com/benthompson/status/968738252944486400), this is a great marketing idea. It looks like a cool game, and it’s 85 percent funded with less than a week to go. I backed it simply to thank Krieger for these terrific design threads.
- [Cabel Sasser mentioned](https://twitter.com/cabel/status/968738894329065473) that Krieger had previously done a similar thread on Walt Disney World’s Tomorrowland. I love Tomorrowland, so I wanted to read that one too.
- [I searched for “max krieger tomorrowland thread”](https://duckduckgo.com/?q=max+krieger+tomorrowland+thread) in my favorite search engine, hoping to find his tweet starting that thread. Instead, the top result gave me something even better: [a collection of five design threads from Krieger](https://threadreaderapp.com/user/MaxKriegerVG) — the new [Metreon one](https://threadreaderapp.com/thread/968609994764705792.html), [Disney Quest](https://threadreaderapp.com/thread/966785570432221186.html), [EPCOT](https://threadreaderapp.com/thread/961296252779552768.html), [Tomorrowland](https://threadreaderapp.com/thread/951497989381656576.html), and [The Cheesecake Factory](https://threadreaderapp.com/thread/931373170791198720.html) (which Krieger describes as “a fully immersive ‘postmodern design hellscape’ themed dining experience”) — on a website I somehow hadn’t heard of before called Thread Reader. Thread Reader does just what you think it does: it collects Twitter threads on a single web page. It’s exactly what I wanted.
- Twitter and the good old fashioned World Wide Web can still be great.
