Back in February, [Basic Apple Guy wrote this terrific post](https://basicappleguy.com/basicappleblog/settingsapp), proposing that System Preferences on MacOS ought to be renamed Settings, to match iOS/iPadOS/WatchOS/tvOS, and redesigned from the ground up to follow iOS’s scrolling-list layout. And then he went further and designed a few detailed mockups to show what he was thinking.


I was going to link to it back then, with a comment along the lines of “*This sounds like a fine idea in theory, and his mockups do look great, but I think in practice it’d be a bad idea.*” But then I thought maybe I should go into detail about why I think it would be a bad idea. And, well, I never got around to turning my notes from that into an actual article — I wound up not even linking to Basic Apple Guy’s piece until now.


Anyway, Basic Apple Guy’s suggestion/prediction turned out to be spot-on. In MacOS 13 Ventura, System Preferences has been replaced by Settings, and it’s not merely a name change, it’s a complete redesign with an iOS-style layout. [Joe Rossignol at MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2022/06/06/macos-ventura-system-settings-app/) and [William Gallagher at AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/22/06/07/apple-simplifies-system-settings-for-macos-ventura-moves-many-items) have good pieces illustrating the new design.


From what I’ve seen in Ventura developer beta 1, I wish I’d just written that short “*sounds like a fine idea in theory but a bad idea in practice*” post back in February. But, after bringing this up during [The Talk Show Live From WWDC 2022](https://daringfireball.net/2022/06/the_talk_show_live_from_wwdc_2022) on Tuesday, hearing Craig Federighi’s take has me more open-minded.
