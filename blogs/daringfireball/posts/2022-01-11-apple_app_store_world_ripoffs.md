A few days ago [I linked to](https://daringfireball.net/linked/2022/01/05/wordle) a New York Times [profile of Josh Wardle](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/01/03/technology/wordle-word-game-creator.html), creator of the delightful and explosively popular web game [Wordle](https://www.powerlanguage.co.uk/wordle/). Wordle, it’s worth noting, has no ads and is free of charge.


As of today, Apple’s App Store is lousy with Wordle rip-offs. I mean not just the concept — there’s a long history of “guess the word” games, including a defunct game show called “[Lingo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lingo_(American_game_show))” that was clearly an inspiration for Wordle — but literally the *name* “Wordle” and its design. [As observed by Greg Karber](https://twitter.com/gregkarber/status/1481001103328088065), as I write this, the #3, #7, #14, and #15 word games in the iOS App Store1 are shameless Wordle clones *stealing the name “Wordle”*. (Worth noting that #16, “[Wordle!](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle/id1095569891)”, was last updated five years ago, is an entirely different timer-based word game, and is simply coincidentally named. There’s also “[Wordle - Word Puzzle](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle-word-puzzle/id1358122111)”, a three-year-old entirely different $2 game that is also coincidentally named.)


Apple, apparently, is just fine with this. [**Update:** As of this evening, all of the following games that used “Wordle” in their name [have been removed](https://www.theverge.com/2022/1/11/22879295/wordle-clones-app-store-removal-online-backlash-copycats) from the App Store.]


A perusal of the rogue’s gallery of Wordle rip-offs:2

- “[Wordle·](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle/id1603184679)”, by Candyblocks, is utterly shameless in its design (and name), but is a free download and apparently has no in-app purchases. Still. (That’s a [Unicode `00B7` “middle dot”](https://www.fileformat.info/info/unicode/char/00b7/index.htm) as a suffix on the app name.)
- “[WRDL](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wrdl/id1604078512)”, by Cole Dennis, is a free game (no IAP) that doesn’t exactly rip off Wordle’s name but does painstakingly copy Wordle’s design, right down to the colors.
- “[Wordle - Daily Word Games!](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle-daily-word-games/id1603978681)”, by Mediaflex Games, is a free app, no IAP, that starts its name with “Wordle” and completely rips off Wordle’s design, colors, icon, and game mechanics.
- “[What Word - Wordle](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle/id1603184679)”, by Rebecca Skinner, is a free game with a $1 IAP to remove ads that includes “Wordle” in its name, and copies Wordle’s color scheme and game mechanics.


And then we get to the real gem of the bunch. “[Wordle - The App](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/wordle-the-app/id1604016922)”, by Zach Shakked, a free-to-download app with a 30-fucking-dollar-per-year “Pro” unlock. Shakked’s rip-off doesn’t just steal Wordle’s name, design, and mechanics, its “The App” suffix clearly was chosen to make it look like the official App Store version of Wardle’s original.3 He even squatted on “@theWordleApp” on Twitter — not *a* Wordle app — *the* Wordle app. Shakked then spent the last day on Twitter giddy with excitement, bragging about how much money his utterly shameless rip-off was making:


> This is absurd. 450 trials at 1am last night, now at 950 and
> getting a new ones every minute. 12K downloads, rank #28 word
> game, and #4 result for “wordle” in the App Store. We’re going to
> the fucking moon.


The [developer and web community](https://twitter.com/dejager/status/1481009967310131203) soon caught on — [Cabel Sasser](https://twitter.com/cabel/status/1480975980608962560), [Rebecca Slatkin](https://twitter.com/RebeccaSlatkin/status/1481000230363488261), [Steven Troughton-Smith](https://twitter.com/stroughtonsmith/status/1480994909016498180), [Jason Kottke](https://twitter.com/jkottke/status/1480971322465435651), and [Andy Baio](https://twitter.com/waxpancake/status/1480990948972658689) all exposed his shameless theft (with screenshots, wisely), and [I simply asked him](https://twitter.com/gruber/status/1480968877953765377), just to be sure, whether he had Josh Wardle’s permission. Within moments, Shakked took his Twitter account (@zachshakked) private. (He left his rip-off app in the App Store, of course, so as not to interrupt its onward trajectory to the moon.)


It gets better, believe it or not. Literally moments before he realized the entire indie developer community was dunking on him and privatized his Twitter account, [this tweet Shakked originally posted back in June surfaced](https://twitter.com/gte/status/1481001654870810626):


> I absolutely despise copycats. Shameless copying is so dumb. Take inspiration from others. Why are they doing that? Why is this a good feature for users? How can we build on top of that?
> Shameless copy/pasting ideas/features will get you nowhere.


in response to one of his other apps having its IAP screen ripped off (and [it was indeed blatantly ripped-off](https://twitter.com/cabel/status/1481009138431709184)) by a rival.


Shakked was wrong about that: shamelessly ripping off Wordle has gotten him somewhere, all right.

2. What about Google’s Play Store? I didn’t hunt for long, but as far as I can tell, there’s just one Wordle rip-off at the moment, and it isn’t high-ranking on the word game leaderboard: “Wordle - Daily Word Challenge”, by Digital Snacks — a free-to-download game with both ads and in-app purchases. I tried it out and deleted it after it rejected “caned” and “paned” — neither obscure — as invalid words. ↩︎︎
3. The actual [official Wordle game](https://www.powerlanguage.co.uk/wordle/) — on the web — works splendidly as an app on iOS with Safari’s “Add to Home Screen” command in the share sheet. The only downside: web apps on the Home screen don’t share cookies with Safari itself, so if you have saved stats you’ll start over. ↩︎︎
