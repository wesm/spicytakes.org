It has come to my attention that I’ll be speaking on a panel Monday
at SXSW, and that I have, as yet, not mentioned this appearance in
this space.


So:


[How to Blog for Money by Learning From Comics](http://2006.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=show&id=IAP060022)


I’m the token weblogger on the panel, and will be speaking alongside
web comic artists [Kevin Cheng](http://www.offpanel.com/), [Bill Barnes](http://www.overduemedia.com/), and [Dave
Kellet](http://www.sheldoncomics.com/); [Nicole Lee](http://2006.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=bio&id=87619) is our moderator.


Our basic premise is that web comic artists have been making money
from their online work for years, and so they have actual experience
regarding what works and what doesn’t.


Through some cruel twist of fate, our panel coincides with “[Design
Eye for the List Guy](http://2006.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=show&id=IAP060020)”, one of three other panels I was most
looking forward to attending. To those of you torn between which one
to attend, however, I say if you’ve seen one redesign, you’ve seen
them all; our shit’s going to be funny and we’re going to set fire to
stacks of hundred dollar bills during the discussion.


(The other two panels I’m most looking forward to are Jeffrey
Zeldman’s “[Roll Your Own Web Conference](http://2006.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=show&id=IAP060068)” and Michael Lopp’s
“[The Five Most Important Startup Decisions](http://2006.sxsw.com/interactive/programming/panels/?action=show&id=IAP060025)”.)


Also, if you’re attending SXSW and happen to be one of the various
DF readers who have ever claimed to quote-unquote “owe me a beer”:
please bring your credit card.
