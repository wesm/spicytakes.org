All great projects begin the same way: by designing a t-shirt.


So it was with Daring Fireball, which I started work on in 1979 as a six-year-old. I didn’t launch the web site for another 23 years, but that’s nothing compared to the original Carter administration-era shirt design, which didn’t make it into production until now.


[

](http://www.coudal.com/swapmeat/swapped.php)


These fine shirts are part of [Coudal Partner’s amazing Swap Meat](http://www.coudal.com/swapmeat/) (don’t forget to check out their [TV commercial](http://coudal.com/index_tv.php)). This is a one-time run of only 75 shirts — 25 each in men’s M, L, and XL. Orders are placed through and shipped from the [Swap Meat store](http://www.coudal.com/swapmeat/swapped.php), but, as with all Daring Fireball t-shirts, each comes with a one-year membership to DF (or a one-year extension for existing members).


(Special thanks to shirt model Ryan Singer, of 37signals.)
