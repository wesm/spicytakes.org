This site has been somewhat of a trailblazing effort in terms of
generating revenue from a weblog via [advertising](https://daringfireball.net/2003/07/independent_days) and via paid
[memberships and T-shirt sales](https://daringfireball.net/2004/06/something_daring). For absolutely no good reason,
however, I’ve never attempted to make some scratch via the most common
means for weblogs: affiliate links.


Starting yesterday, that changed. You will now notice in the top right
corner of this web page a small text link to Amazon.com. For any
purchases made after following this link, Amazon pays me a small
referral bonus. It’s worth noting that the referral bonuses come out of
Amazon’s end, not yours — you pay Amazon’s regular prices, and
Amazon sends a portion (generally around 5 percent) to me.


International readers, take note: I’ve joined the associates programs at
Amazon.ca and Amazon.co.uk as well. Using a pop-up menu on my
[Preferences page](https://daringfireball.net/preferences/), you can choose which store will be linked to
when you visit Daring Fireball. The preferences form also allows you to
turn the Amazon links off, if you choose.


Assuming this endeavor is at least mildly successful, I’ll keep the
links around in some shape or form. The top-right placement in the
banner, however, is something that will only be used for the remainder
of the holiday shopping season.


So, here’s the idea: if you’re going to purchase holiday gifts at Amazon
and you’d like to support Daring Fireball, please consider using these
links to get to Amazon’s web site. After clicking, you’ll proceed to
Amazon’s home page, and all purchases you make in the subsequent 24
hours will generate referral bonuses that benefit Daring Fireball.


A 5 percent cut on the price of a book might not seem like much, but I’m
hopeful it might add up in the aggregate. (Speaking of books,
[*Revolution in the Valley*](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/0596007191/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20), Andy Hertzfeld’s new hardcover on the
creation of the original Macintosh, is now available for purchase.)


More intriguingly, Amazon is a lot more than just a bookstore now. Point
of fact, they’re a terrific Apple reseller — selling a full line of
Macs and iPods, at or below Apple’s own prices. (Unfortunately,
Amazon.ca doesn’t sell Apple hardware, but Amazon.co.uk does.) Sometimes,
Amazon’s prices are *significantly* below Apple’s. E.g., at this writing,
the U.S. Amazon store has the latest iPod models on sale at 5 percent
below Apple’s prices:

- [20 GB iPod U2 Special Edition](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/B0002ZAEYK/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20): $331.55 (save $17.45)
- [40 GB iPod Photo](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/B0002ZAEX6/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20): $474.05 (save $24.95)
- [60 GB iPod Photo](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/B0002ZAEY0/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20): $569.05 (save $29.95)


Or, with Mac hardware, mail-in rebates can save you $100 on iBooks and
iMacs. That brings the price on [a new 12-inch iBook](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/B0002A6YVC/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20) (1.2 GHz G4,
256 MB RAM, built-in AirPort Extreme, etc.) to only $899. Big-spenders
get a $150 rebate on [dual 2.5 GHz PowerMacs](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/asin/B0002A6YBC/ref=nosim/daringfirebal-20).


Even though Amazon caps the affiliate bonus at $25 on computer hardware
purchases, it’s easy to see how this might add up.


Thanks for clicking.


## A Note on URL Bookmarking


If you wish to bookmark my Amazon referral link for quick access within
your browser, you need to bookmark the URL used on *this* page, not the
URL you end up with at Amazon. E.g., for the U.S. store this is the URL
you need to bookmark:


`http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/redirect?tag=daringfirebal-20&path=subst/home/home.html`
