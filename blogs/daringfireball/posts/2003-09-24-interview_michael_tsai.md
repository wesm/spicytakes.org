Michael Tsai is a Macintosh software developer who has authored several useful utilities, including [DropDMG](http://www.c-command.com/dropdmg/) and [BBAutoComplete](http://www.c-command.com/bbautocomplete/). Michael is also the editor and publisher of the monthly Mac e-zine [About This Particular Macintosh](http://www.atpm.com/), and he writes a Mac-oriented [weblog](http://mjtsai.com/blog/).


His latest software release is [SpamSieve 2](http://www.c-command.com/spamsieve/), a major update to his US$25 spam filtering utility. SpamSieve includes several types of filtering strategies, most especially a Bayesian statistical filter. I’ve been using SpamSieve 2 for over a month (including beta releases), and its efficacy has bordered on the incredible: flagging all but 10 of 1981 spams. SpamSieve is easy to set up, runs quickly, and works with every major Mac OS X email client except Apple Mail (it even works with Claris Emailer). Needless to say, I highly recommend SpamSieve.


I interviewed Michael via email.
