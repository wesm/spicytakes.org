“[me.com](http://me.com/)” is currently a placeholder page hosted by [MarkMonitor](http://www.markmonitor.com/), a company that specializes in brand-related DNS management. You can see that it’s handled by MarkMonitor by checking [the whois information for the domain](http://who.is/whois-com/ip-address/me.com/). Apple uses MarkMonitor for most of its domains, [including “apple.com”](http://www.who.is/domain_archive-com/apple.com/), and it’s where they’ve [previously parked domains](http://www.macrumors.com/2008/01/14/macbook-air-domain-names/) for future use.


Additional tidbits:

- [Via Clint Ecker](http://twitter.com/clint/statuses/824317568), the name server for me.com [changed *today*](http://www.domaintools.com/hosting-history/?q=me.com).
- [Via Matthew Yohe](http://twitter.com/mattyohe/statuses/824396144), Netcraft’s [report for “me.com](http://toolbar.netcraft.com/site_report?url=http://www.me.com) indicates that the “DNS admin” for the domain is Apple. This information comes from the DNS SOA record. Type “`nslookup -q=soa me.com`” in Terminal to see this information.
- [Via MacRumors](http://www.macrumors.com/2008/06/01/apple-buys-me-com-macs-new-name/): me.com was the previous name of [Snappville](http://www.snappville.com/), a build-your-own-social-network site, along the lines of Ning. The current me.com placeholder content still redirects to Snappville.


“mobileme.com” [is parked](http://www.who.is/domain_archive-com/mobileme.com/) at MarkMonitor, too, but “me.com” is a bit punchier, no?
