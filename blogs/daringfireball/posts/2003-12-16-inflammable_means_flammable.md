One of the recurring bits of shtick I perform here at Daring Fireball goes like this:

- Some jackass writes something really dumb.
- They get a lot of attention.
- I politely retort.


The problem isn’t with #1. There’s always some new jackass — or [serial offender](https://daringfireball.net/2003/04/qwerty) — publishing some sort of misleading / misinformed / self-aggrandizing crap. The problem is with #2 (which is an essential component in the mix — there’s no reason to argue against someone who’s being ignored), wherein publications which ought to know better give the jerks from #1 unwarranted attention.


What’s nice, though, is that sometimes #3 is unnecessary, because someone else does the retorting.


Such is the case with the recently-reported [DHCP security vulnerability](http://www.carrel.org/dhcp-vuln.html) discovered by William Carrel, and the [bizarrely gloating response](http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,4149,1408953,00.asp) to said vulnerability written by Lance Ulanoff in PC Magazine.


[John C. Welch has written a perfect response](http://www.bynkii.com/archives/2003/12/what_happens_when_you_dont_und.html). Really, I mean it: perfect.
