Today I want to share with you all two recent blog posts from nerdy young women. And who doesn’t love nerdy young women!?


The first is by Michelle G, an M.I.T. student, class of 2018, who is majoring in “14,” which is M.I.T. code for economics. She wrote a blogpost called *[Picture yourself as a stereotypical male](http://mitadmissions.org/blogs/entry/picture-yourself-as-a-stereotypical-male)*, about the question of intelligence, gender, and stereotype threat. I thought I knew all about that stuff but I learned quite a bit from her post. p.s. Larry Summers, I hope you read this.


The second is by Meena Boppana, a Harvard CS major/math minor who [has guest blogged here on mathbabe before](https://mathbabe.org/2015/05/20/gender-and-the-harvard-math-department/). This post is called *[The Making of A Girl Mathlete](https://medium.com/@meenaboppana/the-making-of-a-girl-mathlete-37b001b715e0),* and it describes her experience winning math competitions, often as the only girl. Even though I personally think [math contests kind of suck](https://mathbabe.org/2011/07/17/math-contests-kind-of-suck/), I appreciate how much Meena got out of them.


Speaking of the Harvard math department, I’ll be there next Monday, on a panel with [Moon Duchin](http://mduchin.math.tufts.edu/) talking about gender inclusivity in mathematics. It’s the first of a series. [Here’s the Facebook page of the event](https://www.facebook.com/events/884861171549069/).
