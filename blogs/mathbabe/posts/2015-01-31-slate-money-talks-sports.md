Lots of traveling in the last couple of days, and not enough sleep, has prevented me from posting as often as I’d like. Aunt Pythia sends her regrets.


But if you’re interested, please take a few minutes to listen to [this week’s Slate Money podcast](http://www.slate.com/articles/podcasts/slate_money/2015/01/sports_economics_paying_college_athletes_the_cost_of_sports_stadiums_roger.html), which I particularly enjoyed being part of, and meeting this week’s esteemed guest [Mina Kimes of *ESPN the Magazine*](http://search.espn.go.com/mina-kimes/). We talked about money issues around sports, namely whether college athletes should be paid, the economics of stadiums in cities, and the NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell.


If you’re interested, go [here](http://www.slate.com/articles/podcasts/slate_money/2015/01/sports_economics_paying_college_athletes_the_cost_of_sports_stadiums_roger.html) or look up “Slate Money podcast” on iTunes.
