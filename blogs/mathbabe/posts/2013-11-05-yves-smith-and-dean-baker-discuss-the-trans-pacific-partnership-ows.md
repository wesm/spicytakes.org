Last night I watched [this interview](http://billmoyers.com/segment/yves-smith-and-dean-baker-on-secrets-in-trade/) by Yves Smith and Dean Baker on billmoyers.com. I recommend it for everyone interested in learning about the secret “free trade” agreement currently under negotiation between the U.S. and a bunch of other countries which touch the Pacific Ocean.


[The interview](http://billmoyers.com/segment/yves-smith-and-dean-baker-on-secrets-in-trade/) will explain why “free trade” is in quotes, because it’s really more about protecting corporate interests and extending patents than about reducing obstacles to trade:


As a member of [Alt Banking](http://altbanking.net/), I’m particularly outraged by the financial regulation part of the treaty, which sound like a race to the bottom in terms of common laws between the countries. But probably the worst part of the treaty is related to pharmaceutical protectionism.


Near the end of the interview there’s [an appeal, involving a monetary award](http://org.salsalabs.com/o/1439/content_item/freetpp), for people on the inside to come out and show the world exactly what the contents of the treaty contain. The award is sponsored by [WikiLeaks](http://wikileaks.org/) and is crowdsourced: it currently stands at $61,252. So you can add to it if you want to sweeten the pot.
