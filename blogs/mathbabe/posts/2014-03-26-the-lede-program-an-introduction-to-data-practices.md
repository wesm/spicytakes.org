Speaking of my new job, the website for [the Lede Program: An Introduction to Data Practices](http://www.journalism.columbia.edu/page/1058-the-lede-program-an-introduction-to-data-practices/906) is now live, as is the application. Very cool.


We’re holding information sessions about the program next Monday and Thursday at 1:00pm at the Stabile Center, on the first floor of the Journalism School which is in [Pulitzer Hall](http://www.journalism.columbia.edu/page/274/4). Please join us and please spread the news.


We are also still looking for teachers for the program, and we’ve fixed [the summer classes](http://www.journalism.columbia.edu/page/1060-the-lede-program-courses/908), which will be:

1. Basic computing,
2. Data and databases,
3. Algorithms, and
4. The platform


I’m really excited about all of these but probably most about the last one, where we will investigate biases inherent in data, systems, and platforms and how they affect our understanding of objective truth. Please tell me if you know someone who might be great for teaching any of these, they are intense, seven week classes (either from end of may to mid-July or from mid-July to the end of August) which will meet 3 hours twice a week each.
