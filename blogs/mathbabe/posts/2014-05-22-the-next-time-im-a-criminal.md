Next time I’m tried for a criminal act, I think I’d like to be tried as a big bank.


Then I can pay [a smallish fine](http://dealbook.nytimes.com/2014/05/20/mixed-signals-in-a-corporate-felons-punishment/) for my misdeeds – pocket change for me, a cost of doing business really – and be assured that none of my business partners will stop hanging out with me or stop doing business with me, and in fact [all punishments will likely be waived](http://www.nytimes.com/2014/05/16/business/banks-that-are-criminals-remain-in-business.html?ref=business&_r=0).


In any case, I’d prefer not to be tried as a poor person, where [I’d likely be charged money I don’t have for my free lawyer, for any time I spend in jail, and possibly extra for a full jury](http://www.npr.org/2014/05/19/312158516/increasing-court-fees-punish-the-poor?utm_campaign=storyshare&utm_source=twitter.com&utm_medium=social). And if I didn’t have the money I’d have to spend extra time in jail, until I came up with the money I don’t have.


And since [all people are equal under the law](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equality_before_the_law), even corporations, it shouldn’t matter who I choose to be, so I choose to be a big bank.
