I am somewhat surprised to hear myself say this, but this month’s [Notices of the AMS](http://www.ams.org/notices/201409/) is killing it. Generally speaking I think of it as rather narrowly focused but things seem to be expanding and picking up. Scanning [the list of editors](http://www.ams.org/notices/editors.html), they do seem to have quite a few people that want to address wider public issues that touch and are touched by mathematicians.


First, there’s [an article](http://www.ams.org/notices/201409/rnoti-p1040.pdf) about how the h-rank of an author is basically just the square root of the number of citations for that author. It’s called [*Critique of Hirsch’s Citation Index: A Combinatorial Fermi Problem*](http://www.ams.org/notices/201409/rnoti-p1040.pdf) and it’s written by [Alexander Yong](http://www.math.uiuc.edu/~ayong/). Doesn’t surprised me too much, but there you go, people often fall in love with new fancy metrics that turn out to be simple transformations of old discarded metrics.


Second, and even more interesting to me, there’s an article that explains the mathematical vapidness of a [widely cited social science paper](http://vserver1.cscs.lsa.umich.edu/~spage/pnas.pdf). It’s called *[Does Diversity Trump Ability? An Example of the Misuse of Mathematics in the Social Sciences](http://www.ams.org/notices/201409/rnoti-p1024.pdf) *and it’s written by [Abby Thompson](https://www.math.ucdavis.edu/~thompson/).
