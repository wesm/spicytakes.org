There’s some tricky business going on right now in politics, with a bunch of ridiculous last-minute negotiations to roll back elements of Dodd-Frank and aid Wall Street banks in the current budget deal. Hell, it’s the end of the year, and people are distracted, so the public won’t mind if the banks get formal government backing for their risky trades, right?


[Occupy the SEC](http://www.occupythesec.org/) has a petition you can sign, located [here](http://www.petition2congress.com/17017/petition-against-11th-hour-dilution-dodd-frank/), which is opposed to these changes. You might remember Occupy the SEC for [their incredible work](https://mathbabe.org/2013/12/12/occupy-the-sec-shows-us-all-how-to-occupy/) in public comments on the Dodd-Frank bill in the first place. I urge you to go take a look at [their petition](http://www.petition2congress.com/17017/petition-against-11th-hour-dilution-dodd-frank/) and, if you agree with them, sign it.
