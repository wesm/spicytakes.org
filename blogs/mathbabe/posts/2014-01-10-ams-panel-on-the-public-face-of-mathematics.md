A week from today I’ll be at the [Joint Math Meetings in Baltimore](http://jointmathematicsmeetings.org/jmm) to join a panel discussion on the Public Face of Mathematics. I’ll steal the blurb for my panel from [this page](http://jointmathematicsmeetings.org/meetings/national/jmm2014/2160_amssess):


> *The Public Face of Mathematics, Friday, 2:30 p.m.–4:00 p.m.* Moderated by [Arthur Benjamin](http://www.math.hmc.edu/~benjamin/), Harvey Mudd College. Panelists [Keith Devlin](http://www.stanford.edu/~kdevlin/), Stanford University; [Jerry McNerney](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerry_McNerney), U. S. Congress; [Cathy O’Neil](https://mathbabe.org/about/), Johnson Research Labs; [Tom Siegfried](http://www.tomsiegfried.com/), Freelance Journalist; and [Steve Strogatz](http://www.stevenstrogatz.com/), Cornell University, will share ideas and lead discussion about how the mathematics community can mobilize more members to become proactive in representing mathematics to the general public and to key audiences of leaders in discussions of public policy. Sponsored by the Committee on Science Policy and the Committee on Education.


One thing I’ve already noticed that might make me different from some of the other panelists is that I don’t spend too much time explaining math to the general public, although [my notes on teaching at HCSSiM](https://mathbabe.org/hcssim-2012/) might arguably be the exception. And sometimes [I explain how to do modeling](https://mathbabe.org/2012/06/04/combining-priors-and-downweighting-in-linear-regression/), but that’s not stuff I learned as a mathematician.


Mostly what I do, at least from my perspective, is comment on the culture of mathematics (for [women](https://mathbabe.org/2012/02/06/women-in-math/), for example) or talk about how unfortunate it is that the public’s trust in mathematics and mathematician is being perverted into a political campaign about the (supposed) objectivity of mathematical modeling by [people like Bill Gates](https://mathbabe.org/2013/01/29/bill-gates-is-naive-data-is-not-objective/). I specialize in calling out the misapplication of mathematical imprimatur.
