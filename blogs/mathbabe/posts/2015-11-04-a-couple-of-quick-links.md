I’m in Stockholm, trying desperately to get over jet lag in time for my day-long tutorial tomorrow. Before I go out on the required yarn store walking tour I wanted to share two things with you:


First, I’m super proud of my Occupy group’s recent Huffington Post submission, an essay entitled *[Free Markets Ideology is Making Us Sick](http://www.huffingtonpost.com/the-alternative-banking-group-of-ows/free-markets-ideology_b_8399954.html)*. This is the first of quite a few essays we are planning on the topic, and it’s the driving theme.


Second, I want you to consider listening to [the most recent Slate Money podcast](http://www.slate.com/articles/podcasts/slate_money/2015/11/slate_money_on_payday_loans_postal_banking_and_prepaid_debit_cards.html), where we interviewed University of Georgia Law Professor Mehrsa Baradaran, who recently wrote *[How The Other Half Banks](http://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674286061)*. It’s a fantastic book, and I might write a review of it soon for those people (like me!) who don’t listen to podcasts. Bottomline, we should start a postal bank. Or restart one, rather.
