**Interview on Junk Charts**


Yesterday I was featured on [Kaiser Fung’s Junk Charts blog in an interview](http://junkcharts.typepad.com/numbersruleyourworld/2014/04/numbersense-pros-cathy-oneil-talks-about-trust-in-data-analysis.html) where he kindly refers to me as a “Numbersense Pro”. Previous to this week, my strongest connection with Kaiser Fung was through [Andrew Gelman’s meta-review](http://andrewgelman.com/2012/12/21/two-reviews-of-nate-silvers-new-book-from-kaiser-fung-and-cathy-oneil/) of [my review](https://mathbabe.org/2012/12/20/nate-silver-confuses-cause-and-effect-ends-up-defending-corruption/) and [Kaiser’s review](http://junkcharts.typepad.com/numbersruleyourworld/2012/12/book-review-the-signal-and-the-noise.html) of Nate Silver’s book *The Signal And The Noise*.


**iPython Notebook in Data Journalism**


Speaking of Nate Silver, [Brian Keegan](http://www.brianckeegan.com/), a quantitative social scientist from Northeastern University, recently built [a very cool iPython notebook](http://nbviewer.ipython.org/github/brianckeegan/Bechdel/blob/master/Bechdel_test.ipynb) (hat tip Ben Zaitlen), replete with a blog post in markdown on the need for openness in journalism (also available [here](http://www.brianckeegan.com/2014/04/the-need-for-openness-in-data-journalism/)), which revisited [a fivethirtyeight article](http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/the-dollar-and-cents-case-against-hollywoods-exclusion-of-women/) originally written by Walt Hickey on the subject of women in film. Keegan’s notebook is truly a model of open data journalism, and the underlying analysis is also interesting, so [I hope you have time to read it](http://nbviewer.ipython.org/github/brianckeegan/Bechdel/blob/master/Bechdel_test.ipynb).
