This coming Sunday my friend Adam Reich is coming to Alternative Banking to talk about his work as the faculty director of a collaborative project this summer between [Columbia’s INCITE](http://incite.columbia.edu/) and the [OUR Walmart campaign](http://forrespect.org/).


The plan involves twenty students to scatter across the country, organizing and conducting oral history interviews alongside Walmart workers in five regions.


It is also, not coincidentally, the 50th anniversary of the [Freedom Summer](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_Summer) of 1964, when a bunch of volunteers including students helped register black Mississippians to vote.


Adam is an activist and a sociologist professor at Columbia. He is also an author of three books including *[Selling Our Souls: The Commodification of Hospital Care in the United States](http://press.princeton.edu/titles/10266.html).*


Details are as follows, and I hope you can come:


Where: Room 409 of the International Affairs Building at 118th and Amsterdam.


When: Sunday, June 8th, 2-3pm.
