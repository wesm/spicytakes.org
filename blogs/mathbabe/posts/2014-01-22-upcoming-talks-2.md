A few months ago I gave a talk entitled “Start Your Own Netflix” talk that was part of the [MAA Distinguished Lecture Series](http://www.maa.org/meetings/calendar-events/maa-distinguished-lecture-series), the slides for which are available [here](http://prezi.com/oo4_opdov8r4/?utm_campaign=share&utm_medium=copy) and a short video version [here](http://www.maa.org/meetings/calendar-events/maa-distinguished-lecture-series/lecture-videos).


Today I’m planning to modify that talk so I can give a longer and more technical version of it on Friday morning at the Department of Mathematical Science of [Worcester Polytechnic Institute](http://www.wpi.edu/), where I’ve been invited to speak by [Suzy Weekes](http://www.wpi.edu/academics/facultydir/sw.html).


In about a month I’m going to Berkeley for a week to give a so-called MSRI-Evans talk on Monday, February 24th, at 4pm, thanks to the kind invitation of [Lauren Williams](http://math.berkeley.edu/~williams/). I still haven’t decided whether to give a “The World Is Going To Hell” talk, which would be kind of the technical version of my book (and which [I gave at Harvard’s IQSS](https://mathbabe.org/2013/10/30/harvard-applied-statistics-workshop-today/) recently), or whether I should give yet another version of the Netflix talk, which is cool and technical but not as doomsday. If you’re planning to attend please voice your opinion!


Finally, I’m hoping to join in a meeting of some manifestation of the [Noetherian Ring](http://math.berkeley.edu/~nring/) while I’m at Berkeley. This is a women in math group that was started when I was an undergrad there, back in the middle ages, in something like 1992. It’s where I gave my first and second math talks and there was always free pizza. It really was a great example of how to create a supportive environment for collaborative math.
