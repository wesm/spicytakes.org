I’m super excited to announce that I’m teaming up with Nathan Newman and Frank Pasquale on a newly launched project called [Data Justice](http://www.datajustice.org/) and subtitled *Challenging Rising Exploitation and Economic Inequality from Big Data*.


[Nathan Newman](http://nathannewman.org/) is the director of Data Justice and is a lawyer and policy advocate. You might remember his work with [racial and economic profiling of Google ads](http://www.huffingtonpost.com/nathan-newman/racial-and-economic-profi_b_970451.html). [Frank Pasquale](https://www.law.umaryland.edu/faculty/profiles/faculty.html?facultynum=984) is a law professor at the University of Maryland and the author of a [book I recently reviewed](https://mathbabe.org/2015/01/15/the-black-box-society-by-frank-pasquale/) called [The Black Box Society](http://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674368279).


The mission for Data Justice can be read [here](http://www.datajustice.org/site/mission) and explains how we hope to build a movement on the data justice front by working across various disciplines like law, computer science, and technology. We also have a [blog](http://www.datajustice.org/site/djblog) and a [press release](http://www.datajustice.org/blog/data-justice-report-taking-big-data-platforms-economic-justice-issue) which I hope you have time to read.
