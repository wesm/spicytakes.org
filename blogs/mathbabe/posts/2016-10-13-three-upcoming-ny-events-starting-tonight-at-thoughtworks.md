I’ve got three upcoming New York events I wanted people to know about.


**Thoughtworks/ Data-Pop Alliance Tonight**


First, I’ll be speaking tonight starting at 6:30pm at [Thoughtworks](https://www.thoughtworks.com/locations/new-york), at 99 Madison Ave. It’s co-hosted by [Data-Pop Alliance](http://www.datapopalliance.org/), and after giving a brief talk about my book I’ll be joined for a panel discussion by [Augustin Chaintreau](http://www.cs.columbia.edu/~augustin/) (Columbia University), moderated by [Emmanuel Letouzé](http://datapopalliance.org/bio/emmanuel-letouze/) (Data-Pop Alliance and MIT Media Lab). There will be Q&A as well. More [here](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/weapons-of-math-destruction-does-big-data-threaten-democracy-equality-tickets-28287230869).


**Betaworks next week**


Next I’ll be talking with the folks at Betaworks about my book next Thursday evening, starting at 6:30pm, at 29 Little West 12th Street. You can get more information and register for the event [here](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/brown-bag-with-cathy-oneil-tickets-28400855724).


**Data & Society in two weeks**


Finally, if the world of New York City data hasn’t gotten sick of hearing from me, I’ll be giving a “Databite” (with whiskey!) at [Data & Society](http://datasociety.net/) the afternoon of Wednesday, October 26th, starting at 4pm. Data & Society is located at 36 West 20th Street, 11th Floor. I will update this post with an registration link when I have it.
