Do you guys remember the event I went to last week? It was the kick-off event for a new group at Harvard called Gender Inclusivity in Mathematics (GIIS), and it went well. It was [written up in The Crimson](http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2015/9/15/gender-in-math-series/), together with an action shot of me and Moon giving out tough love advice:


This took place in the same room in which I wrote my quals, which might explain why I’m looking confused and horrified.


One of the organizers, Cherie Hu, [wrote a blog yesterday inspired by last week’s event](https://cheriehu.wordpress.com/2015/09/22/gender-math-music-work-identity/) which ponders identity and mathematics.


Also! I was please to be featured in [a story that ran in Le Monde last week](http://webusers.imj-prg.fr/~leila.schneps/Herzberg2.pdf) with my friend and mathematical colleague [Leila Schneps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leila_Schneps), [who recently wrote a book with her daughter](http://www.nytimes.com/2013/03/27/opinion/when-judges-cant-do-math-justice-suffers.html?_r=0) about bad math in courts systems. It’s in French, obviously, but if I remember my schooling correctly I am quoted in the article talking about destructive algorithms like the Value-Added Model for teachers and even political micro-targeting. No picture there though.
