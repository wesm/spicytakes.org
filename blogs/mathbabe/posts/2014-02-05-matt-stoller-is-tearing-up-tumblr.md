I’ve been super impressed by [Matt Stoller’s recent foray into “tumbling”](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/), which is kind of like blogging except it’s called tumbling. Even the spam emails from tumblr are worth following him, because sometimes they contain his newest posts.


His title is *[Observations on Credit and Surveillance](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/) *but in fact the content is all over the map, reading original source documents to describe the connections between [communism and U.S. slavery](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/75618961543/antebellum-red-baiting-when-slaveholders-accused) or [Gerald Ford and Watergate](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/74989101523/gerald-ford-was-knee-deep-in-the-watergate-cover-up), not to mention the [1894 Post Office Bank proposal](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/75511682474/1894-proposal-to-establish-a-post-office-bank).


Most interesting to me is the information he’s uncovered about [data provisions in the TPP](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/74747093617/zombie-trade-agreements) and the history of [credit cards](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/74429329688/banks-as-big-brother) and [debt collection](http://mattstoller.tumblr.com/post/74197121814/how-the-financial-system-increasingly-resembled-loan).


Go take a look, he’s been on fire. I hope he keeps it going.
