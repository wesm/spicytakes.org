Last Friday I was interviewed for a live NPR show called [To The Point](http://www.kcrw.com/news-culture/shows/to-the-point), hosted by Warren Olney and broadcast by [KCRW in Santa Barbara, California](http://www.kcrw.com/).


The [topic of the show was predictive policing](http://www.kcrw.com/news-culture/shows/to-the-point/can-big-data-prevent-crime); specifically, whether “Big Data” can prevent crime. I was definitely the most skeptical of the four guests. Along with me were Mic.com journalist [Jack Smith IV](http://mic.com/profiles/188814/jack-smith-iv), techno-futurist [Daniel Burrus](http://www.burrus.com/), and [Tom Casady](http://lincoln.ne.gov/city/police/304info.htm), the Public Safety Director from Lincoln, Nebraska.


You can listen to the podcast [here](http://www.kcrw.com/news-culture/shows/to-the-point/can-big-data-prevent-crime).
