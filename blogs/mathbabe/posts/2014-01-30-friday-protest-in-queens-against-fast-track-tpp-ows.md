This coming Friday will be a [coordinated day of action against the Trans Pacific Partnership Agreement (TPP)](http://www.tradejustice.ca/tpp/jan31/). Actions will take place [all over the world](http://www.tradejustice.ca/tpp/jan31/january-31-list-of-actionsevents/) with the New York version taking place at noon in Queens (details below).


**Why anti-TPP? Isn’t “free trade” a good thing? The word “free” is in it!**


Language is a tricky thing, and people choose the names of their initiatives to make them sound good. We know that from the “pro life” and “pro choice” debate.


Turns out that, in this case, “free trade” is a weird phrase to describe a campaign that increases the legal power of corporations (ex: tobacco companies) against governments (ex: Namibia).


I’ve written about it [here](https://mathbabe.org/2013/12/13/international-trade-agreements-and-big-money-bullying-ows/), but if you haven’t seen [this Huffington Post video](http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/01/23/ceo-caveman_n_4652825.html?1390499834) (h/t Matt Stoller) then please take the time. It’s funny and it’s an amazingly clear and beautiful explanation of the dangers of TPP, and especially the so-called “Fast Tracking” of that international agreement.
