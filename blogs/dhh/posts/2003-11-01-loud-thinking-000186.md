Michael, I'm thrilled to see your offering
[ebook](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/B0000TW2Y8/ref=lpr_g_4/104-4903795-3089521?v=glance&s=books)
[versions](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/B0000U7L4I/ref=lpr_g_3/104-4903795-3089521?v=glance&s=books)
of your
[latest book](http://www.michaelmoore.com/books-films/dudewheresmycountry/)
. It makes it
[easier, faster, and cheaper](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000185.html)
to access your voice in Europe and the rest of the world. But I'm very disappointed by the heavy-handed DRM controls burdening the ebook currently available.

The following rights are reserved:
- **Copy: ** *not allowed*
- **Print:** *not allowed*
- **Lend:** *not allowed*
- **Reading aloud:** *not allowed*

What's that all about?! I want to print out a chapter to read in bed and I want to
[copy it to my Nokia phone](http://www.cerience.com/docs/S60/docs/index.htm)
to read on the bus. I can do neither.

Please tell your
[publisher](http://www.twbookmark.com/)
to stop taking away rights that are freely available with the paper version. I'm doing them a
**favor**
by buying a ebook version that has zero marginal distribution and production costs, and they're punishing me for the fact.

That's just plain wrong.

*The complaint above was filed with Michael Moore—author of the just released [Dude, Where's My Country](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0446532231/104-4903795-3089521)—a few minutes ago...*
