Vince has a
[tutorial for getting started with Rails](http://darkhost.mine.nu:81/~vince/rails/tutorial.html)
by writing a todo list  application. It's a very nice step-by-step approach with screenshots and plenty of hand-holding. So if you're just starting out with or considering to give Rails a shot, it's an excellent companion to the
[video](http://media.nextangle.com/rails/rails_setup.mov)
,
[reference documentation](http://api.rubyonrails.org/)
, and
[howtos](http://www.rubyonrails.org/show/Howtos)
.

Once you've gotten a hold on the basics in Rails, you'll want to move on to testing pretty quickly. Testing runs deep with the Rails from the automated testing files generated by both new_model and new_controller generators to the
[wide range of assertions](http://ap.rubyonrails.org/classes/Test/Unit/Assertions.html)
available.

Steve Kellock explains all about it in
[Testing with Ruby on Rails](http://www.swimshark.ca/rails/testing_tutorial.pdf)
. From unit to functional testing, from why to how. It's all there in his 30-paged PDF on the subject. Oh, and it includes a very nice reference documentation on all the assertions as well with all options explained. Awesome work.
