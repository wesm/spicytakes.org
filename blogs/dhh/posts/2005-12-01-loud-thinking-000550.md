I can't believe that its been only two years since I started working with Ruby. So much has happened since that its hard to think that I was actually doing PHP, Java, and other tech stuff for another 5 years before that. In any case, Ruby gave a thousand thoughts a direction and today is a day of celebration for what I began all those months ago:
[Ruby on Rails went 1.0!](http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/articles/2005/12/13/rails-1-0-party-like-its-one-oh-oh)

Not only did we go the big one oh, we also launched a new site designed by Mr. 37signals himself, Jason Fried, which I'm very proud of:
[http://www.rubyonrails.org/](http://www.rubyonrails.org/)
. It includes
[two additional screencasts](http://www.rubyonrails.org/screencasts)
, a very nice list of
[applications in the wild](http://www.rubyonrails.org/applications)
running Rails,
[the core team](http://www.rubyonrails.org/core)
looking sharp, and of course a ton of
[great quotes](http://www.rubyonrails.org/quotes)
from people I admire.

So it's a big day. A great beginning. I can't wait to see where this will all go in 2006.
