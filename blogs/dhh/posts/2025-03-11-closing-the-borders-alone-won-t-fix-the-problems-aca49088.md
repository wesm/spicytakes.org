Denmark has been reaping
[lots of](https://www.irishtimes.com/world/europe/2024/05/29/how-denmarks-social-democrats-stole-far-right-thunder-on-migration/)
[delayed accolades](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/02/24/magazine/denmark-immigration-policy-progressives.html)
from its relatively
[strict immigration policy](https://www.infomigrants.net/en/post/62576/denmark-unprecedented-measures-to-signal-to-migrants-they-are-not-welcome)
lately.
[The Swedes](https://www.thelocal.dk/20230426/we-are-10-15-years-behind-swedish-pm-praises-strict-danish-immigration)
and the Germans in particular are now eager to take inspiration from The Danish Model, given
[their](https://www.dw.com/en/aschaffenburg-what-we-know-about-the-deadly-knife-attack/a-71402933)
[predicaments](https://www.gisreportsonline.com/r/sweden-immigrants-crisis/)
. The very same countries that until recently condemned the lack of
[open-arms/open-border policies](https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/aug/19/sweden-negative-net-immigration-figure-record-low-asylum-application-global-displacement)
they would champion as
[Moral Superpowers](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/09/03/world/europe/sweden-election-populism.html)
.
But even in Denmark, thirty years after the public opposition to mass immigration started getting real political representation, the consequences of culturally-incompatible descendants from
[MENAPT](https://migrant-integration.ec.europa.eu/news/denmark-new-statistics-category-migrants-muslim-countries_en)
continue to stress the high-trust societal model.
Here are just three major cases that's been covered in the Danish media in 2025 alone:
1. Danish public schools are [increasingly struggling with violence and threats](https://www.berlingske.dk/indland/vold-og-trusler-er-blevet-hverdag-i-folkeskoler-naesten-2000-bortvisninger) against students and teachers, primarily from descendants of MENAPT immigrants. In schools with 30% or more immigrants, [violence is twice as prevalent](https://www.berlingske.dk/indland/paa-skoler-i-koebenhavn-med-mange-ikkevestlige-elever-oplever-flere). This is causing a flight to private schools from parents who can afford it (including [some Syrians!](https://www.berlingske.dk/indland/han-er-far-til-tre-og-har-droppet-forraaet-folkeskole-til-fordel-for)). Some teachers are [quitting the profession](https://www.berlingske.dk/indland/hun-blev-truet-af-elever-indtil-hun-sagde-op-det-var-koranen-der-styrede) as a result, saying "the Quran run the class room".
2. Danish women are [increasingly feeling unsafe](https://mim.dk/nyheder/pressemeddelelser-om-ligestilling/2025/marts/ministeren-for-ligestilling-vil-skabe-mere-tryghed-for-kvinder-i-nattelivet) in the nightlife. The mayor of the country's third largest city, Odense, says [he knows why](https://www.bt.dk/politik/borgmester-med-klar-opfordring-til-unge-kvinder-gaa-en-stor-bue-udenom-disse): "It's groups of young men with an immigrant background that's causing it. We might as well be honest about that." But unfortunately, the only suggestion he had to deal with the problem was that "when [the women] meet these groups... they should take a big detour around them".
3. A soccer club from the infamous ghetto area of Vollsmose [got national attention](https://politiken.dk/sport/art10111167/Hyggeseriebold-endte-i-vold-og-trusler.-Nu-er-sagen-havnet-p%C3%A5-kulturministerens-bord) because every other team in their league refused to play them. Due to the team's long history of violent assaults and death threats against opposing teams and referees. Bizarrely leading to the situation were [the team got to the top of its division](https://www.berlingske.dk/indland/flere-fodboldhold-naegter-at-spille-mod-vollsmose-klub-vil-hellere-lade) because they'd "win" every forfeited match.

Problems of this sort have existed in Denmark for well over thirty years. So in a way, none of this should be surprising. But it actually is. Because it shows that
[long-term assimilation just isn't happening](https://x.com/jonatanpallesen/status/1899379119286108620)
at a scale to tackle these problems. In fact, data shows the opposite:
[Descendants of MENAPT immigrants are more likely to be violent and troublesome than their parents](https://integrationsbarometer.dk/tal-og-analyser/filer-tal-og-analyser/arkiv/NotatvedrrendekriminalitetenblandtMENAPT.pdf)
.
That's an explosive point because it blows up the thesis that time will solve these problems. Showing instead that it actually just makes it worse. And then what?
This is particularly pertinent in the analysis of Sweden. After the "far right" party of the Swedish Democrats got into government, the
[new immigrant arrivals have plummeted](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2025/01/17/sweden-s-all-out-tightening-of-migration-policy_6737114_4.html)
. But unfortunately, the net share of immigrants is still increasing, in part because of family reunifications, and thus
[the problems](https://x.com/jonatanpallesen/status/1879929506393444842)
continue.
Meaning even if European countries "close the borders", they're still condemned to deal with the damning effects of maladjusted MENAPT immigrant descendants for decades to come. If the intervention stops there.
There are no easy answers here. Obviously, if you're in a hole, you should stop digging. And Sweden has done just that. But just because you aren't compounding the problem doesn't mean you've found a way out. Denmark proves to be both a positive example of minimizing the digging while also a cautionary tale that the hole is still there.
