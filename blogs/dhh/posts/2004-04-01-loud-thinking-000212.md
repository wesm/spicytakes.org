Charles Miller examines the getter/setter syndrome in Java
from a usage (rather than the normal construction) perspective and
[devices a shortcut](http://fishbowl.pastiche.org/2004/04/04/the_art_of_the_shortcut)
. Instead of using individual access methods, he proposes the unified
that takes a hash of attributes and automatically assigns them on the object instance. Unfortunately, he fails to see its uses and discards the idea with a clever Simpsons reference (
[we like those](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000207.html)
).

That's a shame because he had the right idea.
