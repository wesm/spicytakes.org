Evan Williams (of Blogger fame) and friends have unveiled their latest venture:
[Odeo](http://odeo.com/)
. It's going to make "...it easy for you to discover, create, and subscribe to fresh, independent audio content for your iPod". In short, Portal for Podcasting. That hook got
[the interest of The New York Times](http://www.nytimes.com/auth/login?URI=http://www.nytimes.com/2005/02/25/technology/25podcast.html&OP=22c3274e/sV5LskQ26|8!Q26Q26yQ25sQ25Q5EQ5EAsQ5EQ25sQ25Asy5|@.Q26PQ26Q5BzsQ25ANQ26k|G8yQ3F@yQ5CP)
, but to get the full story, you'll be well advised to read
[Evan's blog posting](http://www.evhead.com/2005/02/how-odeo-happened.asp)
about it and
[the Odeo blog](http://odeo.com/blog/)
in general.

I had a sneak peak at the application a few days ago and it looks mighty cool. I'm a big fan of podcasting myself (especially from the ever excellent
[ITConversations](http://www.itconversations.com)
), so the prospect of Odeo makes me pretty excited.

What also makes me excited is Odeo running Ruby on Rails. Remember that posting
[Evhead advertises for a Rails developer](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000367.html)
back in the beginning of December? That resulted in the hiring of
[Rabble](http://www.blogger.com/profile/111541)
as the lead developer on Odeo. He
[talks about the Odeo launch](http://www.anarchogeek.com/archives/000494.html)
and how they "...built it with the wonderful Ruby on Rails framework" in his blog.

So congratulations, guys! It's great to see another high-profile site launch on Rails and loving it. Keep 'em coming.
