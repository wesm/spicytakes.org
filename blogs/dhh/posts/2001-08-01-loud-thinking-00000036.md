I've recently finished reading
[The Psychology of Computer Programming](http://www.amazon.co.uk/exec/obidos/ASIN/0932633420/qid=997827878/sr=1-4/ref=sr_sp_re_1_4/202-8404512-2290259)
and
[The Mythical Man-Month](http://www.amazon.co.uk/exec/obidos/ASIN/0201835959/o/qid=/sr=8-1/ref=sr_aps_b_1_1/202-8404512-2290259)
. The first celebrating its 30th birth day this year, the second its 25th - meaning that they're both older than me! And yet most of the content feels like it could have been written yesterday. It's truely striking how a profession that has evolved so much in the past couple of decades still haven't changed much at the fundementals.

I strongly recommend reading both of them if you're involved with software development of any kind (but if you're only getting one of them, get the Man-Month). You'll surely be able to relate, and be given amble chance to reflect.
