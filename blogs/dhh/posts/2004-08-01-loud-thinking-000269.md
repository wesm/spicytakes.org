Rails is closing in on the first thousand downloads, which means I've been getting a ton of good feedback on how Rails could be even easier to use and setup. I've taken all that to heart and improve the framework in a number of significant ways.

So if you've been holding back on having a look at the Ruby on Rails phenomenon, now is the time to get on board. As
[Chad Fowler](http://www.chadfowler.com/)
noted yesterday after getting his first Rails application running:
*"This is the police — drop the J2EE and back away slowly"*
. And as former CTO of
[GE Appliances](http://www.geappliances.com/)
in Bangalore and co-author of
[Professional Apache Tomcat](http://www.wrox.com/WileyCDA/WroxTitle/productCd-0764543725,descCd-authorInfo.html)
, he should know.

Come on, then.
[Have a look](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
. You'll be up and running in a few minutes, producing real results within hours, and hanging out with the, at times, 40+ crowd on
[#rubyonrails](http://www.rubyonrails.org/show/IRC)
in no time at all.
