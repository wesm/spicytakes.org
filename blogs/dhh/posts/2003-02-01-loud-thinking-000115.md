Finland
[did yesterday](http://www.effi.org/julkaisut/tiedotteet/pressrelease-2003-01-31.html)
what Denmark could not: Reject the horribly flawed European Union Copyright Directive (EUCD), which makes it illegal to break copyright protections on CD's and DVD's. A pass would mostly outlaw DVD's on Linux and rips of that new Lauryn Hill CD to your iPod.

Denmark is in deep disgrace. We were the only country along with Greece(?!) to pass the EUCD on December 22nd of last year. It's pathetic. From the Finnish coverage, it seems that breaking copy protection under the EUCD is a
*criminal*
offence carrying the maximum penalty of two years in jail. Two years.
