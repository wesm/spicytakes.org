Dave Thomas of the Pragmatic Programmer's have long advocated a break from the
[logical positivistic](http://www.xrefer.com/entry/552659)
view of software development. Along with his partner Andy Hunt, he wrote The Pragmatic Programmer in 2000 that encompassed such metaphysical abstracts as The Broken Window and Don't Repeat Yourself.

But with his latest blog entry
[Artifacting](http://pragprog.com/pragdave/Practices/Artifacting.rdoc,v)
, he's finally committing his allegiance to the world of
[phenomenology](http://www.connect.net/ron/phenom.html)
. Thomas suggests that we look at software development artifacts, such as requirement and design documents, by restating the artifacts as processes, or verbs, instead of results, or nouns. Artifacts, the physical paper product, are an insignificant footnote in comparison to the months of it's creation.
