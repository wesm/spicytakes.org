[Next Angle](http://www.nextangle.com)
, my company site, has been embarrassingly out of date for ages because flat HTML files are just such a pain to maintain. No more! The new site has been relaunched with timely content on the CMS-version of
[Instiki](http://www.instiki.org)
, which makes the back end look just like a wiki — and it's as easy to update as one, too. Since, well, it
*is*
just a wiki.

The new site doesn't yet contain my upcoming
[speaking](http://www.37signals.com/workshop-062504.php)
[engagements](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000219.html)
, but that and the details on my next commercial
[Ruby on Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
project (for a New Zealand company, no less!) will be up soon.

I've kept it really simple, mainly just running off the Instiki base template, with just a dash of CSS to do the two-column setup. Much nicer than my old "designed" boxy thang. Oh, that reminds me that I'm no longer billing myself as a web designer. How can I work with
[these guys](http://www.37signals.com)
on a daily basis and then even contemplate charging money for my own feeble attempts? I just can't do it.
