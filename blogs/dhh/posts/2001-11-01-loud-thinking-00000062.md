My first real programming job as a freelance have involved a fair share of
[regular expressions](http://etext.lib.virginia.edu/helpsheets/regex.html)
, and I constantly found myself working on a mess that would only stay readable in my mind for a few minutes at a time. Once it left, I had to play the decipher game to get it back - wasting several minutes every time.

As I care about code readability, for the sake of maintenance and aesthetics, it troubled me. Especially because I know it doesn't have to be this hard. Simple solution emerge by thinking long and hard. I obviously hadn't thought long or hard enough.

Enter Mark Pilgrim, who
[comments every single step of his expression](http://diveintomark.weblogger.com/2001/11/13)
, syntax and intent included, to keep himself out of trouble. What an astonishingly simple solution.

Thanks Mark, I'm now free to blink and code regular expressions simultanously.
