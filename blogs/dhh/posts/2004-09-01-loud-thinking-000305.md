Despite having a
[presentational video on Rails](http://media.nextangle.com/rails/rails_setup.mov)
that constructs a blogging system in less than 10 minutes, Loud Thinking is still chugging along on a Moveable Type installation that is years old. An installation that does neither Textile nor run of a database for integration with other scripts. It's one of those always-on-the-todo-list kind of things.

Thankfully, not all
[Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
developers are as slow as me to get their weblog running on Rails. Marten and Sarah are both running a home-grown weblogging system built with Rails on
[Standard Behavior](http://www.standardbehaviour.com/log/index)
and
[One Before](http://www.onebefore.org/)
, respectively.

Both systems look really nice and sports pretty designs on top of it. Sarah's system even has a nice
[statistics section](http://www.onebefore.org/statistics/list)
to get an overview of how the blog is doing.

Hopefully Sarah (or Marten) will get around to
[open sourcing](http://rubyforge.org)
of her system, so there's a base to offset my eternal procrastination in getting my blogging on Rails going.

**UPDATE:**
John Wilger also just
[relaunched](http://thatwebthing.com/weblog/2004/09/24/8)
his blog
[ThatWebThing](http://thatwebthing.com/home/)
that now runs Ruby on Rails as well.
