[Dave Thomas](http://pragprog.com/pragdave/)
has been
[touring his Ruby talk](http://pragprog.com/pragdave/Random/RailsAndTextMate.rdoc)
ever since his first stop at Amazon. For his on-the-spot programming, he has been using
[TextMate](http://macromates.com)
about which he notes:
*"...this editor just works for these presentations"*
. Mighty flattering. But Dave has even more up his sleeve.

For the Ruby tutorials, he has been using
[Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
to implement his Amazon application example. I'm not sure how many times he has run the presentation, but something about the feedback he's getting must be pretty convincing:

*It’s generating some buzz—I think Rails may well be the framework to break Ruby into the mainstream.*

I couldn't ask for higher praise or a greater vision for Rails.
