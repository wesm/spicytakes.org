After my
[horrendous](https://dhh.dk/archives/00000076.html)
[experience](https://dhh.dk/archives/00000087.html)
with Sony over a broken hard drive in the old Vaio last year, I've been disillusioned with the support of electronics bought outside the Danish border (since within, there's a mandatory 2-year full-on warranty).

So I wasn't exactly full of hope when I phoned Apple this morning about the battery gone in the iBook I
[bought on my trip around the US](https://dhh.dk/arc/000010.html)
last year. What a surprise I got! Almost instant connection with a Danish-speaking supporter that read me three
[knowledge base](http://www.info.apple.com/)
suggestions (that didn't work) and then concluded he better send me a new battery.

*"It should be there within a few days"*

Not only that. He had all my information on file from the product registration, so I didn't even have to give him my address.

World-wide Warranty actually means exactly that for Apple. This has got to be one of the cleanest interactions I've ever had with any company on warranty issues.

Ready to
[switch](http://www.apple.com/switch/)
, yet? I echo my recommendations for either an
[iBook](http://www.apple.com/ibook/)
or
[iMac](http://www.apple.com/imac/)
(or both!) from a household of two iPods, two iBooks, an iSight, and that glorious iMac I'm typing this from.

(If you're contemplating a PowerBook, keep drooling a little while longer —
[Think Secret](http://www.thinksecret.com/news/augustpowerbooks.html)
and
[PowerPage sources](http://ogrady.blogspot.com/2003_08_01_ogrady_archive.html#106121824808660971)
says new 15" and 17" models could be here as soon as this week!)
