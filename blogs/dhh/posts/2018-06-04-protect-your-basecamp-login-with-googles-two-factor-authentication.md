Using SMS as a second security factor for signing into web applications is [no longer recommended](https://www.theregister.co.uk/2016/12/06/2fa_missed_warning/) by security experts. Therefore **we will be ending our homegrown SMS verification program on July 2nd, 2018, and switching to Google’s state-of-the-art Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) system.**


Moving forward, if you want to secure your Basecamp account with 2FA, you’ll need to log into Basecamp using a Google Sign-In. You can use Google Sign-In with 2FA through [their own authentication app](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/1066447?hl=en), [1Password](https://support.1password.com/one-time-passwords/) (we love those guys!), or the gold standard of [a physical Yubikey](https://www.yubico.com/).


#### How to switch to Google Sign-in with 2FA for Basecamp:

- [Instructions for Basecamp 3 customers](https://3.basecamp-help.com/article/84-logging-in#start-using-your-google-account-to-log-in)
- [Instructions for Basecamp 2 customers](https://2.basecamp-help.com/article/415-log-in-with-google)
- [Instructions for Basecamp Classic customers](https://help.basecamp.com/basecamp/questions/479-how-can-i-switch-to-google-sign-in)


It’s never been more important to take serious precautions to guard your security online. Hacks are common, and failing to protect your online accounts with a second factor makes it so much easier to become a victim. We highly recommend switching to a Google Sign-In so you can take advantage of the protections 2FA provides.


Besides Basecamp, please take the time to get acquainted with two-factor authentication in general, and ensure that you have it turned on for as many services as you can. These days, almost everyone offers some way of adding a second factor. Read about doing it for iCloud, Dropbox, GitHub, Facebook, and Twitter.


Good security is like good backups. It’s a bit of a hassle to setup, but the regret you’ll feel if you don’t have it when you need it dwarfs that inconvenience. Don’t procrastinate.
