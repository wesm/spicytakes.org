When I drive the 24 Hours of Le Mans, I spend a total of about 6-9 hours in the car, divided into stints of roughly two hours at a time. It's intense. But
[talking with Lex Fridman](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ)
in Austin on his podcast? Over six hours straight! We only interrupted the session for five minutes total to take three bathroom breaks. All that endurance training has clearly paid off!
But the magic of a good conversation, like the magic of driving at Le Mans, is that time flies by. Those six hours felt more like sixty minutes. This is what
[flow](https://www.amazon.com/Flow-Psychology-Experience-Perennial-Classics/dp/0061339202/)
does: it compresses the moment.
Besides, we had plenty to talk about. Lex prepares like no other podcast I've ever been on. Pages and pages of notes. Deep questions, endless attention for tangents. We covered
[the beauty of Ruby](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=2715s)
for half an hour alone! But also the
[future of AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=1h44m18s)
,
[small teams](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=2h32m32s)
,
[why we left the cloud](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=3h14m38s)
,
[Elon Musk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=3h33m19s)
,
[fatherhood](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=4h06m22s)
,
[money and happiness](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vagyIcmIGOQ&t=5h53m3s)
, and a million other topics (which Lex mercifully timestamps, so listeners without six hours to spare can hop around).
