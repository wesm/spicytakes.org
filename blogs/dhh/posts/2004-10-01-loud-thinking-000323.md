[RubyGems](http://rubygems.rubyforge.org)
is one of the best things that have emerged while I've been Rubying. It's the equivalent of CPAN + apt-get as a repository for Ruby libraries and applications. It has been gaining speed fast and currently holds 111 distinct gems, which is about 10% of the number that age-old
[Ruby Application Archive](http://raa.ruby-lang.org/)
has in store — impressive and fast take up!

Even more impressively, though, is the uptake among users. Chad has
[just announced](http://www.chadfowler.com/index.cgi/Computing/Programming/Ruby/FiveThousandDownloadsOfRubyGems.rdoc,v)
that more than 5,000 copies of RubyGems have been downloaded and more than 20,000 gems have been retrieved! I'm incredibly proud to see that
[Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
is responsible for at least 1/4 of all gems downloaded.

I can't wait until RubyGems is part of the
[standard library](http://www.ruby-doc.org/stdlib/)
. Now that the release of Ruby 1.8.2 seems to be taking longer than first anticipated, it would be fantastic if RubyGems made it in before release. Are you listening, Matz :)?

While we await inclusion in the standard library, let's make the world of gems even more exciting. I for one would bless anyone that would make these libraries available as gems:
[FastCGI](http://raa.ruby-lang.org/list.rhtml?name=fcgi)
,
[MySQL/Ruby](http://www.tmtm.org/en/mysql/ruby/)
,
[Posgres/Ruby](http://www.postgresql.jp/interfaces/ruby/)
. Those three are currently the only non-gem libraries I have installed.
