Among friends, it's been no secret that my new Nokia 3650 has me more than a little enthralled. There's just so much to love. The astonishing
[picture quality](https://dhh.dk/mblog)
from the tiny camera, a perfect email-client speaking IMAP over GRPS and doing attachments with ease, decades of retro-gaming with the
[GameBoy](http://www.wildpalm.co.uk/GoBoy7650.html)
and
[C64](http://koti.mbnet.fi/~haviital/)
emulators, a sweet calendar, and browsing with mobile Opera. All in a single, slick package for around $300.

And now Apple just blessed it with iSync-compatibility!
[Version 1.1](http://www.apple.com/isync/)
adds address book (with pictures) synchronization and promises calendar (the most important thing) and to-do lists "soon". Hopefully "soon" is defined as in days or weeks, not months.

On top of that,
[QuickTime 6.3](http://www.apple.com/quicktime/download/)
allows for a
[3GPP plug-in](http://www.apple.com/mpeg4/3gpp/)
, so the movies recorded from the camera phone can be played-back outside of Nokia's closed, Windows-based player. That is freaking awesome. Prepare to see mobile movie-blogging rocket.

The
[Nokia 3650](http://www.nokia.com/nokia/0,,2273,00.html)
is the phone to own.
