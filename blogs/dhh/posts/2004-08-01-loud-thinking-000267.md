Even before the complete
[Ruby on Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
[experience](http://media.nextangle.com/rails/rails_setup.mov)
was unveiled, the OpenACS guys were longing to get
[Active Record running in Tcl](http://openacs.org/forums/message-view?message_id=182169)
. Shortly after the experience went public, Pythonists were considering the making of
[Python on Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org/show/PythonOnRails)
.

Now, not much more than a week after the first release, M. Veldthuis wants to have
[Rails.NET](http://www.standardbehaviour.com/Log/index.php?p=2)
, or
*Track*
as he has chosen to label it. It's certainly flattering if only a little befuddling: Why not try the real thing out for size first?
[Ruby on Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
is here, today, offering the rapid development experience free of charge.

If it's the language, Ruby, that you have doubts about taking on, I promise you it'll be
*a lot*
less work to
[Getting Started with Ruby](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000199.html)
and running on Rails than it will be to recreate the sensation in another language on another platform.

Heck, I was just writing the other day about Apple's Ben Stiglitz who picked up Ruby in a week and wrote the solid beginnings of a bug tracker in Rails within
*three hours*
.
