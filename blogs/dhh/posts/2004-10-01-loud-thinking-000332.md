As
[earlier mentioned](http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000322.html)
, the fine chaps at
[Combustion Labs](http://www.combustionlabs.com/)
invited me to come to Vancouver for a week to introduce their team of PHP programmers to
[Ruby on Rails](http://www.rubyonrails.org)
.

Now considering that all three companies I've done Ruby on Rails consulting for has either already switched or anticipates to switch to the platform, I feel that the pressure is on to keep the winning streak going.

And I wonder when the bag of tricks is going to run out, so it won't be possible to dazzle guys like Lars Pind of
[Collaboraid](http://www.collaboraid.biz/)
who
[noted this](http://pinds.com/blog/one-entry?entry%5fid=20989)
after I visited:

*Watching David demonstrated to me directly, visually, personally, a very productive way of working that broke with a number of traditions we've held for years...*

So to ease my mind, I'm planning to think of stuff I'd like to do on whatever little spare time I might have. Do you have any good suggestions?

I'm flying in on Sunday and going out on Saturday. (Going out as in flying to San Francisco for
[Building of Basecamp III](http://www.37signals.com/workshop-basecamp.php)
— do consider
[signing up](http://www.adaptivepath.com/cgi-bin/regapp/reg.cgi)
if you're in the area, there's still a few seats left).
