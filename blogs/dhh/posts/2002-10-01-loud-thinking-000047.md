The Microsoft marketing people likes the
[Apple Switch campaign](http://www.apple.com/switch/)
. So much they've sought to
[mimic it](http://www.scripting.com/misc/msSwitchAd/gustavson.png)
(in reverse, of course). Ironically, they didn't care enough about the real users of Windows XP to actually find one to say they liked it better than a Mac. Instead, they prepped a
[random model photo](http://creative.gettyimages.com/source/search/detail.asp?source=searchResults&imageIndex=10&hdnSync=%22One+Woman+Only%3AOnly+Women%22+and+%22Coffee%3AHot+Drink%22~0%2C12%2C449%2C3%2C15%2C1%2C0%2C0%2C0%2C12287%2C0%2C1%2C0%2C0%2C0%2C0%2C0%2C0%2C0%2Cen-us%2C1%2C%22One+Woman+Only%3AOnly+Women%22+and+%22Coffee%3AHot+Drink%22%2C389%2C257%2C1%2C&hdnCurrPage=4)
with some stock lyrics on "demanding the best in mobile computing".

The text is so inclusive, it's embarrassing. The "must trump all crusades in each chant" spread is so thick you can taste it. Office XP Is The King Suite. Netscape Bows To All-Mighty The Explorer. Yawn!

![](https://dhh.dk/lt-files/fiess.jpg)

People care about people. Not about puppets dancing in obvious and predictable patterns. We want
[the stoned](http://www.apple.com/switch/ads/ellenfeiss.html)
,
[the euforic](http://www.apple.com/switch/ads/fabiolatorres.html)
,
[the scary](http://www.apple.com/switch/ads/hamiltonmorris.html)
, and
[the cool](http://www.apple.com/switch/ads/djqbert.html)
.

Yet another nilch on the Microsoft score card.
