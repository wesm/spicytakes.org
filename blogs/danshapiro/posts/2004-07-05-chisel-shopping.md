Time to buy myself some non-horrible chisels.  I was over at [Scott’s](http://wannabe.guru.org/scott/) house using his crosscut sled (something I haven’t had time to build yet).  He had a set of [Irwin/Marples Blue Chip chisels](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0000224EV/personworks-20/) straight from Home Depot.  These are your classic “eh” tool.  They’re not total crap, but they’re not the [chisels you’re going to take to your grave](http://www.traditionalwoodworker.com/catalog/product_info.php?cPath=39_92_338&products_id=575) by any means.  The handles are plastic, the metal and quality control are so-so, and they’re generally adequate.

I used the 3/4″ to clean up a cut that was a bit ragged.  As soon as the blade hit the wood fibers I was brutally reminded that *my* chisels are absolute, unmitigated, [crap](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B00002X1YO/personworks-20/).  Not only that, but my wife recently used them to chip some cement out of a crack in the tiles.  Frankly, at this point I’d be better off with a butter knife.  They’re new and the blades aren’t chipped (and proposing this alternative to my wife will prevent any complaints when I actually do buy a set of chisels).

So I did some homework, and I’m sharing the results with you.  [This](http://www.woodcentral.com/bparticles/chisel_review.pdf) is a pretty good wrap up of some nice chisels and comparisons; mostly useful as a checklist of things to consider when you decide what’s right for you.  [This](http://www.capeforge.com/Chisel%20Names.html) is a good summary of chiselspeak.

Now comes the decision: how much to spend, and what chisels to buy with it?  I decided to set myself an arbitrary budget of around $60.  Here’s what I considered.

1) Get a set of [4 Blue Chip chisels](http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B0000457K2/personworks-20/) and blow the extra $35 on a good bottle of wine.

2) Get a set of [4 Crown bench chisels](http://www.traditionalwoodworker.com/catalog/product_info.php?cPath=39_92_213&products_id=1986).  Mm, pretty rosewood handles.

3) Get [two](http://www.traditionalwoodworker.com/catalog/product_info.php?cPath=39_92_212&products_id=905) [badass](http://www.traditionalwoodworker.com/catalog/product_info.php?cPath=39_92_212&products_id=906) chisels that will last forever, and deal with only having 1/4 and 3/4 sizes

4) Ditto, but they’re [turning](http://japanwoodworker.com/product.asp?s=JapanWoodworker&pf_id=14.300.06&dept_id=11572) [japanese](http://japanwoodworker.com/product.asp?s=JapanWoodworker&pf_id=14.300.18&dept_id=11572) (These are Fine Woodworking’s #1 rated chisels).  Note that these are a buck or two cheaper [here](http://www.craftsmanstudio.com).

Hm, will have to chew on this for a bit… I’ll repost when I have chisels in hand & let you know how things turned out.


(Not that my wife complains when I buy woodworking tools–seriously.  She’s awesome.)
