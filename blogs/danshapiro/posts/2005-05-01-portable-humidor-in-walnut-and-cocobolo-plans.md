It’s done!  Eleven pages of careful detail explaining how YOU can make a portable humidor just like the one pictured below.  The 4 megabyte PDF file has detailed instructions, including diagrams and photos.  It’s a straightforward project and the results are fantastic.



[Download the plans in Adobe Acrobat format here](https://www.danshapiro.com/blog/MiscArticleAttachments/Portable%20Humdior.pdf).

I’m also trying something a little different.  I’ve included the source materials that I used to make this: the [publisher file with the layout](https://www.danshapiro.com/blog/MiscArticleAttachments/Portable%20Humidor.pub), the [text in plain .txt format](https://www.danshapiro.com/blog/MiscArticleAttachments/Portador%20Humidor%20Text.txt), all the [pictures in full resolution in a giant 20 MB zip file](https://www.danshapiro.com/blog/MiscArticleAttachments/Portable%20Humidor-sources.zip), and even the [3d image file](https://www.danshapiro.com/blog/MiscArticleAttachments/humid.skp) I used to render the images (you need [this software](http://www.sketchup.com/) to read it).  If you want, you “remix” this any way you choose–translate it to ancient greek, fix my typos, or use the pictures as a background for an adult-oriented woodworking site.  I have no idea why anyone would want to do this, but there you have it.  See the second page of the plans for the details of how this works–it’s licensed with a [Creative Commons](http://creativecommons.org/) license that allows all this and more.

One more thing–if you do make one, post a comment or send me a picture!  I’ll thank you for it.
