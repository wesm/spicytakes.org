I’ve had a peaceful, quiet little twitter account since 2006.  Then one day, on a lark, I threw up [@danshapiro](http://www.twitter.com/danshapiro) up during a [talk that I  was giving about RC airplanes](http://bit.ly/9mkLYx). The talk got [boingboinged](http://boingboing.net/2009/10/21/a-geeky-introduction.html), and my inbox flooded with random folks  following me.  (Hi!)  Ever since, I’ve had a couple of new follows a  day.  Ashton, here I  come.


So here’s the (blurry) algorithm I use to decide who to follow:


![](http://www.danshapiro.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/Mytwitterfollowalgorithm-241x300.png)

*If you're a friend, or your followers outnumber your follows, I'll look at your page.  If you don't tweet too often and at least a third of your tweets are interesting (and private exchanges with someone do not qualify), then I follow you.*


So that’s my decision tree.


What’s yours?
