Keeping this pretty short because I’m writing this at Data Day Texas, which I consider to be the best gathering of data nerds in the US. Free of vendor-shilling, Data Day is a chance to catch up with old friends, make new ones, drink and enjoy BBQ, and nerd out on data and AI. The challenge is there are so many great talks, and omnipresence isn’t a reality. Oh well. The close and informal nature of the event reminds me of something I’ve been ranting to friends - community matters more than ever.


In an age of endless AI slop, toxic social media, internetenshitification, and anepidemic of loneliness, communities (especially in person) are an antidote. I suspect that as AI continues unrelenting into every area of life, people will seek something else. I predict people will gravitate to strong communities more than ever as people tire of algorithmically generated relationships. Spending time with my friends at Data Day Texas reminds me of the power of just…hanging out.


Speaking of communities. In case you missed it, thePractical Data Discordcommunity is now open to the wider public. I had it in beta for a couple of weeks, and things went well. Now, we have a fast growing community of passionate data and AI practitioners and leaders.


What can you expect from the Practical Data Community?

- A positive and supportive place to discuss and collaborate on ideas, practices, and more. By practitioners, for practitioners.
- Book clubs and learning opportunities.
- Events, both on Discord and elsewhere.
- I’m thinking of doing a Practical Data Conference in 2025. Stay tuned for that.
- No vendor pitches. This is a place for genuine discussions, not gratuitous sales pitches.


Thanks to everyone who joined the community. It means a lot!


Have a wonderful weekend,


Joe
