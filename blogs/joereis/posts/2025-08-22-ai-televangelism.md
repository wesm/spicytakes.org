The AI Bubble (and yes, it’s a bubble) feels a lot like late-night televangelism. You’ve got preachers on stage telling us the Lord AGI is coming down from the sky any day now. But salvation is expensive, so please send in your checks. TheRapture of the Nerdsis at hand. Can I get an Amen?


I’ve lived through a few bubbles, and this one is different mostly in scale. The expectations are galactic, the money astronomical. It makes the dot-com era look almost thrifty.


Lately, though, the mood is shifting. Sam Altman is suddenly more careful in his language. Mustafa Suleyman has moved from AGI to the hedged phraseSeemingly Conscious AI. Even Eric Schmidt is telling people tochill out about AGI. When the evangelists start hedging, you know the revival tent is getting drafty.


GPT-5’s launch was kind of a dud. Incremental progress, but not AGI like it was hyped to be last year.


Then came MIT’snew report: 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots show no measurable ROI. Headlines screamed “AI fails in business.” The nuance is different. Pilots in back-office automation are working, especially with specialized vendors (~67% success), but in-house experiments mostly flopped. It’s a snapshot, not a death sentence for AI. But you wouldn’t know that from the headlines. The markets are reacting though, with drops throughout the week before the Fed said today they’re open to lowering interest rates.


Meanwhile, companies keep laying people off “because AI.” Which is it? If AI pilots are failing everywhere, it can’t also be replacing half the workforce overnight. You can’t have both “AI doesn’t work” and “AI is coming for your job” at the same time. The crossed signals are dizzying.


What’s really happening is familiar: AI is running into the same walls as every modernization effort before it - messy organizations, unclear success metrics, and slow adoption curves. But when you mix in trillion-dollar infrastructure bets, weak revenue from AI companies, and whispers that AI spending is propping up the economy, people start asking whether the story is outpacing reality.
