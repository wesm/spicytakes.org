0:00
-7:28

I’m keeping this short (I’m traveling around Europe this weekend) but I want to share a pattern I can’t ignore.


Wherever I go, I see two camps:

1. The Builders. They’re exploring what AI can do for their organizations and their personal lives. Often ruthlessly brute forcing AI into every workflow to understand how it can benefit them.
2. The Holdouts, aka AI Denialists. They’re on the fence, or outright denialists. These are unapologetically labeledflat‑earthers.


Over the past month, I’ve watched non‑technical teams around the world embrace AI, either through their employer’s approved tools or the “shadow AI” they bring from home (ChatGPT or whatever they prefer). The outcome is consistent. The ones who experiment and integrate AI into real workflows are getting outsized results. The impact is even greater with technical teams. I don’t see a scenario where people go back to pre-genAI. It’s not gonna happen by choice.


Meanwhile, the fence‑sitters…are still on the fence. Some call AI evil. Others insist it’s useless and they work faster without it. Maybe. But often, they’re repeating the same year of experience twenty times and calling it twenty years. Oh well, not my problem.


Things change when you go AI‑Native. AI shifts the unit of time. I routinely compress weeks of work into days. The point isn’t a precise multiplier; it’s that the curve bends. ever forward, even with maximum pressure.


My workflow is now AI‑native. I use AI as a primary tool to solve business problems end‑to‑end. I’m talking strategy, research, drafting, iteration, QA, writing and deploying code. In the past, I had to rely on people for much of this. People are great (myself included :). But as a small business, I want to stretch as far as I can with technology. With the big AIs (hi Claude, GPT, Gemini), it feels like having a bench of co‑founders. We strategize, build, and execute with serious velocity. Calling that a hoax or a fad is, at best, misinformed.


Scared money don’t make money. The biggest tsunami you’ve ever imagined is approaching your business, your careers, and you personally. If you remain a spectator, the wave won’t slow down for you. You’re going to get crushed beyong recognition. Today you have a choice - learn to ride it or get swamped. I’m betting on the builders. Everyone else is too late and f’d.


For now, have a fun weekend,


Joe
