Welcome to the Weekend Windup, a new edition to my Substack. Each weekend (Saturday and Sunday will vary depending on travel and such), I’ll feature some cool things I’ve read, and some awesome events I’m either participating in, or very stoked on.


Today, I’m playing with using Claude to automate tasks in Apple Reminders (I’m a Boomer). I use the Reminders app constantly, and as I look through the tasks, I realize most of the tasks could be handled in part or in full by AI. So, if I have a reminder to research a task, I can just set a reminder that will trigger Claude to work on my behalf. I guess I could just ask Claude to do it too, but I prefer having my AI’s run multiple tasks in the background if possible, as this frees up the app for interactive chats. In general, the ability to chain multiple apps together and have AI orchestrate tasks and workflows is pretty damn cool. It’s not always perfect (the AI’s time out a lot and don’t finish tasks), but I expect the models and capabilities will improve a ton.


Other than that, doing dad stuff like shuttling kids to weight lifting and football games.


Also, hopefully finishing the draft Part 2 ofHow Organizations Shape Your Data Modelthis weekend, with the aim to publish it sometime next week. For the book, I’m aiming to have the manuscript done in the next few weeks, with Book 1 (focused on the Fundamentals of Data Modeling) targeted for publication in late January 2026. Will keep you posted when pre-orders are available.


For now, have a fun weekend,
