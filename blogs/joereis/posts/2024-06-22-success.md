I just wrapped up a great Maven course, Transition from Data Practitioner to Effective Leader, which I taught alongside award-winning data executiveSol Rashidi. We taught a cohort of people motivated to level up their careers, and we learned just as much from them as they learned from us. These are my favorite forms of value exchange.


One thing that stood out is how people view “success.” Very often, career success is about moving up in title and rank. Based on the conversations (some might say therapy sessions) I have with many people in high-title/rank positions, the lamentation I hear is, “Is this it? Is this what I worked hard to achieve?” If this is the only path to success, we might need to rethink what success means.


I’ve seen “successful” people take a number of paths, including the following.

- Title
- Prestige
- Influence
- Money
- Helping others


There are infinitely more versions of success than these. My big point to the class was to find the path to success that works for you. There’s no “right” path you can take. The corporate executive route was never for me. Yet I’m having a lot of fun and consider myself “successful.” Success, to me, means impacting a lot of people with education and thought leadership.


You have your path and definition of success. Go that way.
