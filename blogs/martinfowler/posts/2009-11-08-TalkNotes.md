I give a lot of talks, and like any speaker I'm always asked for
  a copy of my slides. Also like many speakers, particularly those who
  put some thought into their slides, I hate giving out copies of
  slides as handouts. When I use slides, I design them to be a
  [VisualChannel](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/VisualChannel.html): an
  accompaniment to my words, not as a stand-alone â[Slideument](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/Slideument.html)â. Such
  slideuments are very distracting when talking, as reading the bullet
  points takes the audience's attention away from the words being
  spoken. As a result my slides are graphic, minimal, and often
  involve animation that's cued into my talking. Such slides are
  meaningless without me speaking.


A written piece is a much more effective stand-alone document. I
  can write this to make sense without audio, and use an approach
  designed for reading rather projecting on a screen.


So here's some suggested items to read that go with recent topics
  I've been talking about. I think most people will find them much
  more useful than slideuments.


## NoSQL and Consistency


This material is a summary of Chapter 5 of our book [NoSQL Distilled](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/NosqlDistilled.html)


## Agile Essence and Fluency


The first half of this talk is based on my essay [The New
    Methodology](https://martinfowler.com/articles/newMethodology.html). In the second part I move into discussing Larsen
    and Shore's [Agile Fluency
    model](https://martinfowler.com/articles/agileFluency.html).


## The Value of Software Design


The starting point of this talk is the
    [TradableQualityHypothesis](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/TradableQualityHypothesis.html). I then explain the 
    [DesignStaminaHypothesis](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/DesignStaminaHypothesis.html) leading into the metaphor
    of [TechnicalDebt](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/TechnicalDebt.html). The last part of the talk is the
    description of  the forms of technical
    debt using the [TechnicalDebtQuadrant](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/TechnicalDebtQuadrant.html).


## Domain-Specific Languages


I have a [guide
  page](https://martinfowler.com/dsl.html) that acts as a central point for material I 
  post on DSLs. In particular the state machine example I use in the
  talk comes from the [first chapter of my book](http://www.informit.com/articles/article.aspx?p=1592379), which is available as
  a free download.


## Continuous Integration and Delivery


The best starting for more on this is my [guide page](https://martinfowler.com/dsl.html) on software
    delivery. The first part of the talk contrasts [feature
    branching](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/FeatureBranch.html) and [continuous
    integration](https://martinfowler.com/articles/continuousIntegration.html). The second part looks at deployment pipelines and
    continuous delivery, for which the best resource is the book by [Humble and
    Farley](https://martinfowler.com/books/continuousDelivery.html).


## Richardson Maturity Model


This talk is based on [my
  article](https://martinfowler.com/articles/richardsonMaturityModel.html).


## Two Kinds of Software Projects


This talk looks at the contrast between what I call strategic
    and utility software projects. I've written about  this in a [bliki
    entry](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/UtilityVsStrategicDichotomy.html).


## Schemaless Data Structures


This talk is based on [my infodeck](https://martinfowler.com/articles/schemaless/).


## Ten years since the Agile Manifesto


This is still very much an extemporary talk, and I haven't put
  together any writing that mirrors it yet. For more background you
  can read:

- my article on the [writing of the
    manifesto](https://martinfowler.com/articles/agileStory.html)
- my bliki entries on [Semantic Diffusion](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/SemanticDiffusion.html) and [craftsmanship
    and the crevasse](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/CraftmanshipAndTheCrevasse.html)
- my SD times interview [Agile at 10](http://www.sdtimes.com/link/35489)
- and of course [the
  manifesto itself](http://agilemanifesto.org/).


## Non-Deterministic Tests


This is based on my article on [Eradicating
    Non-Determinism in Tests](https://martinfowler.com/articles/nonDeterminism.html)


## Event Sourcing


I described the basic idea of [event sourcing as a pattern](https://martinfowler.com/eaaDev/EventSourcing.html) a few
    years ago. During the talk I mention the [LMAX architecture](https://martinfowler.com/articles/lmax.html) as
    an example of using a [MemoryImage](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/MemoryImage.html) and also mention [CQRS](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/CQRS.html).


## Introduction to NoSQL


For more of my writings on NoSQL, take a look at [this list](https://martinfowler.com/noSQL.html). In particular
    our [infodeck on NoSQL and Polyglot Persistance](https://martinfowler.com/articles/nosql-intro) is a good overview
    of why we think this topic is important. For more depth on this,
    take a look at [our
    book](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/NosqlDistilled.html).


## NoSQL Models


The best resource for more information on NoSQL models is [our
    book](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/NosqlDistilled.html). Beyond that I also talk about [aggregate-orientation on
    my bliki](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/AggregateOrientedDatabase.html).


## Multitudes of Mobiles


This talk has a good online representation in the form of my
    infodeck on [Developing Software for Multiple Mobile Devices](https://martinfowler.com/articles/multiMobile/).


## The Essence of Agile


This talk is mostly based on my essay [The New
    Methodology](https://martinfowler.com/articles/newMethodology.html). During the talk I also talk about the necessity
    of [practices for
    evolutionary design](https://martinfowler.com/articles/designDead.html) to avoid the danger of
    [FlaccidScrum](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/FlaccidScrum.html).
