November 2025 marked an important point in the development of AI-assisted
  programming. In that month Anthropic released Opus 4.5 and OpenAI released
  GPT-5.2. The former in particular, coupled with the Claude Code harness,
  represented a key moment for using AI.


> one of those moments where the models get incrementally better in a way
>     that tips across an invisible capability line where suddenly a whole bunch
>     of much harder coding problems open up
> -- [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jan/4/inflection/)


> Sometime in late 2025, I wrote my final lines of code. I wasn’t aware of
>     it at the time, but that day marked a transition after almost four decades
>     of programming. I’ve created and shipped plenty of code since then, but with
>     one key difference: that code wasn’t written by hand. It was written by AI
>     agents that I directed.
> -- [Adam Tornhill](https://adamtornhill.substack.com/p/welcome-to-code-for-humans-and-machines)


The change was reinforced by the Christmas break. For a couple of weeks
  people who were too busy with their day job to play with AI had the
  opportunity to try out these new models.
