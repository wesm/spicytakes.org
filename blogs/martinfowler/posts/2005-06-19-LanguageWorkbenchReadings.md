When I wrote my recent article on [Language Workbenches](https://martinfowler.com/articles/languageWorkbench.html), I decided
	to separate the section on further reading into my bliki, to make it
	easier to report updates when new stuff appears.


For the last few years **Intentional Software** has been
			hiding under some deep rocks. They are beginning to peep out
			now. The main site for more on their work is [http://www.intentsoft.com](http://www.intentsoft.com/). In particular it's worth keeping an eye on their [blog](http://blog.intentsoft.com/). Perhaps the best description of the Microsoft research is in chapter 11 of [Czarnecki and Eisenecker](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0201309777/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=0201309777&linkCode=as2&tag=martinfowlerc-20).


JetBrains have an active community and I expect this will
			continue for their [Meta Programming System](http://www.jetbrains.com/mps). Sergey
			Dmitriev, the leader of MPS, wrote an [excellent summary](http://www.onboard.jetbrains.com/is1/articles/04/10/lop/) of his
			vision for MPS and Language Oriented Programming.


As one would expect from Microsoft there's plenty to find on
			**Software Factories**. The [main page for information](http://msdn.microsoft.com/architecture/overview/softwarefactories/) is on
			MSDN. It's also worth checking out various blogs: [Steve Cook](http://blogs.msdn.com/stevecook),
			[Jack Greenfield](http://blogs.msdn.com/jackgr/)[Stuart Kent](http://blogs.msdn.com/stuart_kent/), [Keith Short](http://blogs.msdn.com/keith_short/), and 
			[Alan
			Wills](http://weblogs.asp.net/Alan_Cameron_Wills). Tracing through the Software Factory blogs will also give you some
			insights as to the debates over the use of MDA, since much of
			that discussion has been around the Microsoft work.


Speaking of MDA, there are a number of organizations trying to
	build something like a Language Workbench using OMG standards. I haven't looked
	into any of these in any detail. One such organization is [Xactium](http://xactium.com).


My colleague Neal Ford has [ported the initial example of the
	paper to Java](http://memeagora.blogspot.com/2005/07/language-workbench-sample-ported-to.html) and provided a download for the code.


[Rainer Joswig](http://lispm.dyndns.org/news?ID=NEWS-2005-07-08-1) discusses doing this in lisp and even has a video.


For more references on general DSL and Language Oriented
	Programming material good starting points are this [overview](http://compose.labri.fr/documentation/dsl/) and this
	[annotated bibliography](http://homepages.cwi.nl/~arie/papers/dslbib/).


I intend to keep an eye on this field and expect to post
			further material on [martinfowler.com](https://martinfowler.com).
