I recently decided to upgrade my home [MediaServer](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/MediaServer.html) and
  got an [HP N36L](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004G608JW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B004G608JW&linkCode=as2&tag=martinfowlerc-20). So far I'm happy
  with it.


![](images/hpn36l.jpg)


My first reason for the upgrade was to upgrade from using IDE
 drives on my old server. This box makes it very easy to change SATA
 hard drives. There's a door at the front which allows easy access to
 the hard drive bays. You have to mount the hard drive in a runner,
 but that's just a couple of screws.


My second reason was to reduce power consumption. The old server
  drew 80W at idle (as measured by my [Kill A Watt](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00009MDBU/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=B00009MDBU&linkCode=as2&tag=martinfowlerc-20)). The new one only uses 20W,
  which is a pretty dramatic reduction. At $6 a month, I'm not going
  to be making a great ROI on this, but at least I'll feel more
  virtuous about leaving it on all the time.


The box is small and quiet, not a big factor for me as it lives
  in the basement, but that may appeal to plenty of others. I also
  enjoyed the humor of the vendor in including a 160GB drive in the
  package. (I put in something rather larger.)


I installed Ubuntu 10.04 LTS and it worked just fine. For this
  install I took the opportunity to use [puppet](http://projects.puppetlabs.com/projects/puppet) - overkill for a home
  server but useful to give me some devops creds.


Overall something worth recommending but nothing too dramatic -
  but then I don't like drama in a
  server.
