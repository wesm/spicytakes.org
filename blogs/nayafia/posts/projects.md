# [Advanced meditation](https://nadia.xyz/advanced-meditation)


I’m currently exploring a new wave of advanced meditation that offers access to deeper, intensely altered mental states – comparable to psychedelics, but induced through concentration alone – beyond mindfulness. Little is understood about the underlying mechanics, but these practices have potential therapeutic benefits, as well as insight into the mind and consciousness itself.

# [The Silicon Valley ideology](https://nadia.xyz/tech-culture)


I became interested in tech’s growing social and political influence, and the values driving its behavior, after noticing a change in sentiment in the early 2020s. Tech had shifted from primarily building and funding startups to building movements and engaging with public institutions. I wanted to understand why this was happening from a systems perspective; key events that drove this change; and how tech’s story rhymes with prior historical industry wealth booms.


[Read more](https://nadia.xyz/tech-culture).


# [Open source software](https://nadia.xyz/open-source)


I spent five years researching and writing about open source developers. My early work argued that open source code is a form of public infrastructure that requires maintenance (see [*Roads and Bridges: The Unseen Labor Behind Our Digital Infrastructure*](https://www.fordfoundation.org/work/learning/research-reports/roads-and-bridges-the-unseen-labor-behind-our-digital-infrastructure/)). My later work focused on documenting modern open source (2010s-today) as distinct from early open source (1990s-early 2000s) and drawing parallels to the creator economy (see [*Working in Public: The Making and Maintenance of Open Source Software*](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0578675862/)).


The experience of pursuing this topic across a few different gigs also gave me a blueprint for understanding myself as an independent researcher, a process that I’ve also tried to document in public to hopefully encourage others to do the same.


[Read more](https://nadia.xyz/open-source).


# [Microgrants](https://www.heliumgrant.org/)


I’ve had a longtime interest in modern (post-Industrial Revolution) philanthropy. I wanted to experiment with faster grantmaking processes, as well as funding individuals rather than organizations, so I decided to make my own microgrants program.


Helium Grants were “no-strings-attached” microgrants ranging from $1K to $5K, awarded to individuals. I ran this experiment for two years, and I documented my process and learnings along the way. I also put together a few resources to encourage more people to spin up their own microgrant programs.


[Read more](https://www.heliumgrant.org/).
