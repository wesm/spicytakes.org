I’m happy to announce that my friend, teacher, and competitor Eric Sink has agreed to host the new [Business of Software forum](http://discuss.joelonsoftware.com/?biz).


Eric has been in the software industry from [time immemorial](http://www.google.com/groups?q=+%22Eric+Sink%22&start=70&hl=en&lr=&ie=UTF-8&scoring=d&selm=1990Jun18.183853.27093%40cbnewsj.att.com&rnum=71). He built one of the first web browsers, [Spyglass](http://www.netvalley.com/archives/mirrors/eric/Eric_Weblog.htm) and founded the [AbiWord](http://www.abisource.com/) project to make an open source word processor. His latest company is [SourceGear](http://www.sourcegear.com/), a highly successful [Inc 500](http://www.inc.com/app/inc500/viewCompany.jsp?cmpId=2002156) software company in Champaign, Illinois which makes great source control management software. He writes a column on the business of software for [MSDN](http://msdn.microsoft.com/) and has [his own weblog](http://software.ericsink.com/) where he has been extremely generous with his knowledge of software business. I couldn’t have a better host.
