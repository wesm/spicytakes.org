Due to the poor sound quality of the previous [CityDesk online demo](http://www.paxlogica.com/cdFull.html), I decided to invest in a real studio quality microphone instead of using one of those cheap computer headset/mike combinations. It took me a while to figure out what I needed. The mike itself is a [Shure SM58](http://www.shure.com/microphones/models/sm58.asp), probably one of the most popular professional microphones in use today and generally available for about $100.  I bought the Mic from [Sam Ash](http://www.samashmusic.com) on 48th street, hoping that they would be able to get me the right combination of cables and adapters I needed to plug this thing into a standard sound card. The stoner DJ sales dude sounded very confident but he didn’t tell me that I needed a preamp, and he gave me the wrong kind of cables. If you’re trying to do this yourself, here’s exactly what I have:  the Shure SM58 microphone  A basic desk stand. The clip part that connects the mike to the stand comes with the mike.  a 3′ mic cable (it only needs to reach the preamp). I bought a CBI LowZ Microphone Cable from [Zzounds](http://www.zzounds.com).  A preamp. This boosts the level of the microphone to something that is called “Line Level” which is what a computer sound card needs. I got an [M-Audio AudioBuddy](http://www.m-audio.com/products/m-audio/audibudy.php) from Zzounds.  To connect the preamp to the sound card, you need a cable with a 1/4″ stereo phone jack on one end and a 1/8″ stereo “mini” phone jack on the other end. I assembled this out of two cables which I bought at my neighborhood Radio Shack. For some reason the professional music stores like Sam Ash and Zzounds think it is beneath their dignity to stock any parts with 1/8″ jacks, but that is what your sound card needs. The sound quality is really quite a bit better.
