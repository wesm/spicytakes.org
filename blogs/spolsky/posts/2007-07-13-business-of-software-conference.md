The [Business of Software conference](http://www.businessofsoftware.org/) coming up at the end of October is new this year, but it’s got a pretty phenomenal line-up of speakers:

- [Guy Kawasaki](http://blog.guykawasaki.com/) practically invented the field of high tech evangelism working at Apple in the 1980s, and has written some of the best [books](http://www.guykawasaki.com/books/index.shtml) about marketing I’ve read (my personal favorite is [The Macintosh Way](http://www.guykawasaki.com/books/macway.shtml).)
- Rick Chapman wrote the encyclopedia of software marketing, [The Product Marketing Handbook for Software](http://www.aegis-resources.com/), and the hilarious* *[In Search of Stupidity](http://www.insearchofstupidity.com/), which makes the quite convincing argument that success in software is a matter of just not doing stupid things. ([My foreword to In Search of Stupidity](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/articles/Stupidity.html)).
- [Hugh MacLeod](http://www.gapingvoid.com/) does those great [cartoons](http://www.gapingvoid.com/Moveable_Type/archives/cat_cartoon.html), which is reason enough to listen to him, but he’s also the marketing strategist for the only Web 2.0 wine company.
- [Tim Lister](http://www.systemsguild.com/GuildSite/TRL/Tim_Lister.html) needs no introduction to this crowd. He’s the author of [Peopleware](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/navLinks/fog0000000262.html), the first and last thing you have to read about managing software teams. That’s really all you need to know.
- [Eric Sink](http://software.ericsink.com/), moderator of our own [Business of Software forum](http://discuss.joelonsoftware.com/?biz), co-founder of [SourceGear](http://www.sourcegear.com/), the classic small software company success story.


Also speaking: Dan Nunan, Jennifer Aaker, Jeffrey Pfeffer, Bill Buxton, and me. [Register here](http://www.businessofsoftware.org/).
