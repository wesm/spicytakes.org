[Clay](http://many.corante.com/archives/2007/07/20/spolsky_on_blog_comments_scale_matters.php): “…the sites that suffer most from anonymous postings and drivel are the ones operating at large scale. If you are operating below that scale, comments can be quite good, in a way not replicable in any ‘everyone post to their own blog’”.


[Dave](http://www.scripting.com/stories/2007/07/20/thanksToJoelSpolsky.html): “…he says that I don’t allow comments on Scripting News. That’s not exactly true, there are comments here, but you have to look carefully to find them. I find this ups the quality enormously — people don’t generally comment here to embarass anyone or to provoke a fight — there isn’t enough traffic to interest those people. But the people who want to add information to a thread here on Scripting News, and have been reading the site long enough to know what it’s about, they find their way to the comments and add something to the mix.”


Both excellent comments. Thank you. This is an example of people posting their replies on their own site. There is a lot more value to them than the [comments](http://discuss.joelonsoftware.com/default.asp?joel.3.522299.3) about this post on my own discussion forum. Why? Because I know who Clay is, I’ve met him, he wrote [A Group Is Its Own Worst Enemy](http://www.shirky.com/writings/group_enemy.html), which, to this date, is the most important, insightful, and brilliant understanding of group dynamics in online communities. Dave has something to add to the conversation; some thoughts he’s had since the article I quoted him on. Great content, in their own spaces.


And you’re not only hearing their replies because I’m telling you about them. You might be hearing about it from [Techmeme](http://www.techmeme.com/), which has really cool algorithms to figure out where the conversation is, and put it back together. Look at Techmeme right now:


They reconstructed the conversation magically for you. Of course, Techmeme only works for the biggest, noisiest bloggers. But [Bloglines will do it for anyone](http://www.bloglines.com/search?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.joelonsoftware.com%2Fitems%2F2007%2F07%2F20.html&ql=en&s=r&pop=l&news=m).
