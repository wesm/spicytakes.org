**Ron Vogl** writes:  I did an internship at Microsoft in the summer of 1997, and at that time, Microsoft had two source-code control tools in use.  Some groups did indeed use source-safe.  The Outlook group, of which I was a temporary member, used an internal Microsoft tool called Source Library Manager, abbreviated SLM and pronounced either slim or slam depending on how you were feeling about it.  SLM was command-line only, with a mix of SourceSafe- and CVS-style workings.  From what I could gather from the interns in other groups, SourceSafe and SLM were used in roughly equal amounts.Yeah, I used SLM in the old days. Given how devoted Microsoft is to [eating your own dog food](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/articles/fog0000000012.html), the fact that there are still groups that don’t use SourceSafe says something about how bad that product must be.
