We’re starting to do a lot more Ajax development here. The first Ajax book is out (in ebook form while the printers work on the dead-trees edition.) It’s called [Foundations of Ajax](http://www.apress.com/book/bookDisplay.html?bID=10042), by Ryan Asleson and Nathaniel T. Schutta. Ajax applications are “StartFragment highly dynamic web applications that are nearly as feature-rich as their thick client brethren. Using Ajax techniques, we can provide our customers the rich user experience they have come to expect while still enjoying the ease of deployment that we’ve come to expect.” All those cool features in GMail, Google Maps, Flickr, and Oddpost that make them feel much more responsive than typical web applications are thanks to Ajax techniques.
