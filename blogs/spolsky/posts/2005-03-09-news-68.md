I was quoted in [an eWeek story](http://www.eweek.com/print_article2/0,2533,a=147391,00.asp) about [the VB6 petition](http://classicvb.org/petition/) today: “And this is how Microsoft will lose their desktop monopoly: because some bright bulb at Microsoft thought Boolean operations should really short-circuit, no matter what millions of BASIC developers had been doing since the 1960s.”


**Correction!** This is a bad example, since the boolean operators I was thinking of (**And** and **Or**) were *not* changed to short circuit in VB.Net. I have no idea why I’ve been thinking that they were for so long. There are [other, real examples](http://vb.mvps.org/vfred/breaks.asp) of incompatibilities between VB and VB.NET, but short circuiting was not one of them.
