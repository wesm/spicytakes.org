So I was checking today whether [Daily Bingo Cards](http://www.dailybingocards.com) has started to pick up any search traffic, and aside from the name it is starting to rank for some long tail queries, including #5 on “[bingo cards make yourself](http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&q=bingo+cards+make+yourself)“.  I was kind of suprised that it did that, because while that is certainly a [snowflake query](http://microisvjournal.wordpress.com/2007/05/30/ranking-for-an-arbitrary-organic-search-query) it is one that I have seen before on my other sites.  So I Googled it to see if there was any major competition for the term, and yep, there apparently is — me, myself, and I…


This is not overwhelmingly significant from a commercial standpoint, since there are only about 10 of that particular snowflake every month.  However, I get eight of them, which provides me with a bit of amusement.  I also chuckled that, for at least a minute on an insignificant query, I totally controlled the above-the-fold part of a Google SERP.


Multiply this by a couple thousand different snowflakes and you have my marketing strategy for Daily Bingo Cards in a nutshell.  Fifty billion snowflakes all I need is one…
